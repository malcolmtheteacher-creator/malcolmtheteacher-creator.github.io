Quick Reference: How to Spot the Essay Type

Opinion Essay

  • "To what extent do you agree or disagree?"
  • "Do you agree or disagree?"
  • "What is your opinion?"

Discussion Essay

  • "Discuss both views and give your own opinion."
  • "Some people believe X. Others argue Y."

Problem/Solution

  • "What are the causes of...?"
  • "What measures could be taken to solve...?"

Advantages/Disadvantages

  • "Discuss the advantages and disadvantages."
  • "Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?"

Two-Part Question

  • "Why does X happen?"
  • "What effects does X have?"
  • Two separate question marks in the task

Band 7+ Principles (Apply to Every Essay Type)

Paraphrase the question. Never copy words from the task into your introduction.
Topic sentence first. The first sentence of each body paragraph must state the argument clearly.
Explain, don't just state. After every argument, explain WHY it matters.
Specificity over vagueness. "London's congestion charge reduced traffic by 30%" beats "the government should act."
Use advanced structures. Inversion, nominalization, and complex conditionals push you from Band 6 to Band 7+.
Word count 250-290. Under 250 = automatic penalty. Over 300 = time risk. Aim for 260-280.
