C2 Consulting English

Beyond Perfect

When excellent English isn't enough

C2 | 6 Lessons | For professionals who need to be understood, not just correct

The Problem

Your grammar is near-flawless. Your vocabulary is extensive. You've passed every exam. And yet — sometimes people don't quite follow you. Sometimes your point doesn't land. Sometimes you say exactly the right thing, and it still doesn't work.

"The search for certainty is the biggest obstacle to finding truth."

This course borrows an idea from Kenneth Hammond's Beyond Rationality: that wisdom isn't about eliminating uncertainty — it's about navigating it well. The same is true of advanced English. Mastery isn't perfection. It's knowing when to be precise and when to be approximate. When to hedge and when to commit. When your words are the problem — and when it's everything around your words that's failing.

The Lessons

🔍
01 Register
Where Your Words Come From
English has three layers: Saxon, French, and Latin/Greek. Most speakers use them unconsciously. At C2, you learn to switch between them deliberately — choosing the right layer for the right audience, the right moment, the right effect.
Target Language

Register-switching • Code-shifting between formality levels • Strategic word choice for consulting contexts

 ⚖
02 Calibration
The Language of Not Knowing
Consultants live in uncertainty. You rarely have all the data. The difference between a good consultant and a great one isn't knowing more — it's being precise about what you don't know. This lesson gives you the language to calibrate certainty like an instrument.
Target Language

Epistemic modality • Graduated certainty markers • Calibrated hedging vs vague hedging

 💬
03 Pragmatics
Why They Don't Understand You
Your English is excellent. So why do people sometimes look blank? This lesson tackles the invisible layer of communication: what you imply vs what they infer, cultural assumptions, and the gap between grammatical accuracy and real-world clarity.
Target Language

Comprehension checking • Reformulation strategies • Signposting • Managing implicature

 🎯
04 Strategy
The Power of Imprecision
Sometimes "around 40%" is more powerful than "precisely 38.7%". Strategic vagueness is a skill — knowing when to soften, when to leave space, when a rough answer serves your audience better than an exact one.
Target Language

Strategic vagueness • Deliberate softening • Face-saving language • Constructive ambiguity

 💡
05 Discourse
Thinking Out Loud
In consulting, you often have to process complex ideas while speaking — not before. This lesson gives you the tools to think in English out loud: self-correcting, building ideas in real time, and bringing others into your thinking process.
Target Language

Real-time processing markers • Self-correction • Collaborative idea-building • Discourse management

 🏆
06 Capstone
The Consultant's Edge
The capstone. A high-pressure consulting simulation where you deploy everything: register-switching, calibrated uncertainty, pragmatic clarity, strategic vagueness, and real-time thinking. Present, persuade, pivot — under pressure.
Target Language

All previous language systems under pressure • Integration drills • Consulting roleplay
