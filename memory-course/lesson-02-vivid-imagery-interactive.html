SMASHIN' SCOPE: 12 Principles for Vivid Images

Boring images fade. Vivid, bizarre images stick forever. Use these 12 principles when creating memory images:

Substitution

Replace one thing with another

Movement

Add action and motion

Action

Make things DO something

Size

Make things HUGE or tiny

Humour

Make it funny or silly

Imagination

Break reality rules

Numbers

Use quantities

Senses

Engage all 5 senses

Colour

Use vivid colors

Order

Unusual positions/locations

Positive

Pleasant over unpleasant

Exaggeration

Overdo everything

The Rule: Combine Principles!

One principle is good. Two principles are better. Three or more = unforgettable. Example:

Weak Image:

A pen on a desk

(Only one thing happening)

STRONG Image:

A GIANT pen, 10 feet tall, made of GOLD (Size, Colour, Exaggeration), SPINNING in the air, shooting INK everywhere (Movement, Action), making you LAUGH because it's so ridiculous (Humour)

(Size + Colour + Exaggeration + Movement + Action + Humour = UNFORGETTABLE)

Elaboration: Why Does SMASHIN' SCOPE Work?

Understanding the science behind these principles makes you better at using them.

Question 1: Why does exaggeration work?

Why is a GIANT elephant more memorable than a normal elephant?

Sample answer:

Our brains are wired to notice unusual things. A normal elephant blends in. A giant elephant violates expectations, triggers surprise and attention, which strengthens encoding. Bizarre = Memorable.

Question 2: Why combine multiple principles?

Why is a giant spinning golden pen better than just a big pen?

Sample answer:

Each principle engages different brain systems. Size activates spatial cortex. Movement activates motor cortex. Colour activates visual cortex. Gold activates semantic meaning (wealth, value). More brain systems activated = more retrieval paths = easier recall.
