Key Principle Recall

1. What is the Spacing Effect?

Spacing effect: Learning is enhanced when study sessions are separated by increasing time intervals rather than massed practice.

2. What is Active Recall?

Active recall: Retrieving information from memory without looking at the source material strengthens memory pathways.

3. How does consistency improve memory?

Regular practice rewires neural pathways. Consistency builds automaticity and improves long-term retention.

Spaced Repetition Schedule Design

The Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve: We forget most information rapidly after learning. Strategic spacing fights this:

  • First review: 1 day after learning
  • Second review: 3 days after first review
  • Third review: 1 week after second review
  • Fourth review: 1 month after third review
  • Maintenance: Every 3-6 months thereafter

Daily Routine Architecture

Optimal practice session structure:

Warm-up (5 min): Quick drill on familiar material

Review (10 min): Active recall on previously learned items

New Learning (20 min): Introduce new techniques or material

Application (10 min): Practice techniques on fresh challenges

Reflection (5 min): Journal progress and insights

Maintenance Calendar Strategy

Different memory items need different refresh schedules:

  • Weekly: Fast refresher drills (10-15 min)
  • Monthly: Comprehensive review and new challenges
  • Quarterly: Assessment and goal adjustment
  • Annually: Complete system evaluation and planning

Long-Term System Planning

Sustainable mastery requires thinking in months and years:

  • Months 1-3: Foundation building with consistent daily practice
  • Months 4-6: Technique specialization based on interests
  • Months 7-12: Competitive participation or advanced applications
  • Year 2+: Mastery maintenance and teaching others
