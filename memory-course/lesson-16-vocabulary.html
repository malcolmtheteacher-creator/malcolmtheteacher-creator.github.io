The Keyword Method for Vocabulary

The Keyword Method: A powerful technique for learning foreign vocabulary by finding sound-alike English words, then creating vivid mental images.

3-Step Process

Step 1: Identify the Keyword

Find an English word that sounds similar to the foreign word.

Spanish "Cabeza" (head) sounds like "cab" → Keyword: CAB

Step 2: Know the Meaning

Understand what the foreign word means before creating your image.

Step 3: Create a Vivid Image

Link keyword to meaning with an EXAGGERATED, BIZARRE mental image!

Imagine a GIANT CAB instead of a human head, honking wildly!
Key Principle: Make images as BIZARRE and SENSORY-RICH as possible. Tiny, quiet images fade fast!

Elaboration: Deeper Learning

Why This Method Works

Sound Bridge

The keyword connects foreign sound to familiar English vocabulary you already know.

Visual Memory

Your brain remembers images far better than abstract words.

Active Creation

Creating the image yourself embeds it deeper than passive reading.

Pro Tips for Success

Tip 1: EXAGGERATE EVERYTHING

Images should be BIGGER, BRIGHTER, NOISIER than reality!

Instead of: "A small book"
Imagine: "A BOOK THE SIZE OF A BUILDING, glowing neon colors!"

Tip 2: Engage Multiple Senses

See, hear, feel, smell, and taste your images!

PERRO (Dog): Feel the fur, hear barking, smell dog breath, taste treats!

Tip 3: Use Movement and Action

Make things move, dance, jump, and explode!
