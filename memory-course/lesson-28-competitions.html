New Content: 10 Competition Disciplines Overview

The 10 Standard Memory Competition Events

1. Speed Cards

Time: 60 seconds

Memorize a complete deck of 52 playing cards in the fastest time possible. World record: under 13 seconds!

2. Speed Numbers

Time: 60 seconds

Memorize as many random digits as possible in one minute. Top competitors: 300+ digits.

3. Names & Faces

Time: 15 minutes

Memorize the names and faces of people in photos. Recall must match faces to correct names.

4. Random Words

Time: 15 minutes

Memorize a list of 300 random, unrelated words in proper order.

5. Spoken Numbers

Time: Variable

Memorize numbers spoken at a pace of one per second, then recall all in order.

6. Historic Dates

Time: 5 minutes

Memorize 100 historical events with their dates. Must recall both event and year.

7. Binary Digits

Time: 30 minutes

Memorize long sequences of 1s and 0s. World record: 5000+ digits in 30 minutes.

8. Abstract Images

Time: 15 minutes

Memorize a grid of abstract patterns and shapes. Recall requires visual precision.

9. Hour Cards

Time: 60 minutes

Memorize multiple decks of cards. The ultimate endurance test combining speed and volume.

10. Random Images

Time: 15 minutes

Memorize a sequence of 30 random, unrelated photographs.

Competition Scoring and Rules

  • Speed events: Lowest time wins (with accuracy requirements)
  • Time events: Most correct items within time limit
  • Ranking: Points awarded based on placement, highest total wins
  • Tiebreakers: Usually determined by combination of events or head-to-head
