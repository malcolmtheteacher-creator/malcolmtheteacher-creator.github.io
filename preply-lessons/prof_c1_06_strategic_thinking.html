Strategic Frameworks

SWOT Analysis

SWOT is a practical tool for evaluating your competitive position:

  • Strengths: Internal advantages (brand reputation, skilled workforce, technology)
  • Weaknesses: Internal disadvantages (limited budget, outdated processes, lack of expertise)
  • Opportunities: External positive factors (market growth, new technologies, regulatory changes)
  • Threats: External risks (new competitors, economic downturns, changing customer preferences)

Porter's Five Forces

This framework helps you understand industry competition:

  • Supplier Power: How much control suppliers have over prices and quality
  • Buyer Power: How much control customers have over prices (high power = competitive markets)
  • Competitive Rivalry: The intensity of competition in the industry
  • Threat of New Entrants: How easy it is for new companies to enter the market
  • Threat of Substitutes: The availability of alternative products or services

Strategic Fit Questions

Ask these questions before committing to a strategy:

  • Does this align with our core mission and values?
  • Do we have the resources and capability to execute?
  • What is the realistic ROI and timeline?
  • How does this strengthen or weaken our competitive position?
  • What are the risks if this fails?

Analysis Practice

Analyse this business scenario using what you've learned.

Scenario: Local Bookstore in Decline

An independent bookstore has operated for 20 years in a city centre. Sales have declined by 40% in the last two years. The owner has identified these facts:

  • Amazon offers convenience and lower prices
  • A nearby university brings 30,000 students annually
  • The store has a loyal community following on social media
  • Rent is increasing, making profit margins very thin
  • The owner is passionate about literature and has strong relationships with local authors
  • Most students now read on e-readers

Question 1: SWOT Analysis

Complete a SWOT analysis for this bookstore. What are the key internal and external factors?

Strengths: Loyal community, local author relationships, established brand, passionate owner
Weaknesses: Declining sales, thin profit margins, high overhead costs, limited product range
Opportunities: University market, events and author readings, community hub positioning, book clubs
Threats: Amazon competition, e-reader adoption, rising rent, changing consumer habits

Question 2: Strategic Pivot Options

What strategic pivots could save this business? Consider: shifting to a community hub model, focusing on rare/collectible books, hosting events, creating a café, partnering with the university.

Option 1 – Community Hub: Transform into an event space with authors, book clubs, and literary discussions. Revenue from events, café, and curated book selections.

Option 2 – Niche Market: Focus on rare, signed, and out-of-print books. Target collectors willing to pay premium prices.

Option 3 – University Partnership: Become the unofficial campus bookstore for humanities students. Stock course-related titles, study guides, and literary analysis.

Option 4 – Hybrid Model: Combine online sales with a smaller physical space; reduce overhead while maintaining community presence.

Question 3: Your Recommendation

Which strategy has the strongest value proposition? Why? Consider competitive advantage, scalability, and strategic fit.

Speaking Task: The Turnaround Pitch

Time Remaining: 10:00

Your Task

Imagine you are a business consultant. You have been hired to present a strategic direction to save the bookstore. You have 10 minutes to:

  1. State the core problem clearly
  2. Present one strategic pivot you recommend (SWOT-based)
  3. Explain the competitive advantage of your strategy
  4. Outline the first 90 days of implementation
  5. Address one key risk and how you'd mitigate it

Language Support

Opening your strategy:

  • "The core issue is that..."
  • "Our strategic recommendation focuses on..."
  • "This positions us to..."

Highlighting competitive advantage:

  • "Unlike our competitors, we can...
  • "This gives us a sustainable advantage because..."
  • "The value proposition for customers is..."

Addressing risks:

  • "We acknowledge that... could be a risk."
  • "To mitigate this, we will..."
  • "We've built in checkpoints to..."
