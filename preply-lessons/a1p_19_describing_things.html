üí¨ Two Ways to Describe: "It's..." vs "It looks..."

Both are correct, but they mean slightly different things:

Pattern 1: IT'S + Adjective

IT'S + Adjective = Fact or personal opinion

  • "It's big." (I know it's big)
  • "It's beautiful." (My opinion)
  • "The weather is cold." (Fact)

Pattern 2: IT LOOKS + Adjective

IT LOOKS + Adjective = Based on appearance (I'm guessing)

  • "It looks expensive." (From what I see, it seems expensive)
  • "That food looks delicious." (It looks tasty, but I haven't tasted it)
  • "The movie looks interesting." (From the trailer, it seems interesting)

Quick Rule:

Use IT'S when you KNOW for sure.

Use IT LOOKS when you're GUESSING from appearance.
