üí™ Abilities with "Can"

"Can" means you have the ability to do something.

Examples of abilities:

  • "I can swim." (I have the skill to swim)
  • "She can play piano." (She has the skill)
  • "I can speak English." (I have the language ability)
  • "Can you drive?" (Do you have the ability?)

"Can't" means you DON'T have the ability.

  • "I can't swim." (I don't have this skill)
  • "He can't speak French." (He doesn't have the ability)
  • "They can't cook." (They lack this skill)

But also, "CAN" is used for REQUESTS:

  • "Can you help me?" (A polite request)
  • "Can I have a coffee?" (Asking for permission/requesting something)
  • "Can you open the door?" (Polite request for help)

üìö The Structure of "Can"

One of the easiest modal verbs!

SUBJECT CAN/CAN'T BASE VERB EXAMPLE
I, You, He, She, It, We, They can / can't swim, speak, cook, help... "I can swim"

Why is "can" special?

  • It's the SAME for all pronouns (no "can't" variation!)
  • You NEVER add -S or -ED
  • It's followed by the BASE VERB (can swim, NOT can swims)
  • Negative is "can't" (NOT "cannot" in speaking)
Can you...?
For QUESTIONS and REQUESTS
"Can you help me?" / "Can you speak English?"
I can...
For POSITIVE abilities
"I can swim." / "I can speak French."
I can't...
For NEGATIVE abilities
"I can't cook." / "I can't drive."

üí¨ Making Polite Requests with "Can"

In English, "Can you...?" is one of the MOST COMMON polite requests.

Polite request patterns:

  • "Can you help me?" ‚Äî Asking for help
  • "Can I have a coffee?" ‚Äî Asking for something
  • "Can you speak English?" ‚Äî Checking ability
  • "Can I use your pen?" ‚Äî Asking permission

Responses to "Can you...?" questions:

Yes: "Yes, I can." / "Yes, of course!" / "Sure, I can help."

No: "No, I can't." / "Sorry, I can't." / "I'm afraid I can't."

Can I...?
For asking PERMISSION
"Can I have the menu?" / "Can I sit here?"
Can you...?
For POLITE REQUESTS
"Can you pass the salt?" / "Can you help me, please?"

ü§ù Guided Practice: I Do ‚Üí We Do ‚Üí You Do

STEP 1: I DO (Teacher Model)

Question: What can you do? What can't you do?

My answer: "I can speak English well. I can cook traditional food. But I can't dance!"

Notice: Subject + Can/Can't + Base Verb!

STEP 2: WE DO (Teacher + Student Together)

I ask, you answer:

Teacher: "Can you swim?"

Possible responses: "Yes, I can." OR "No, I can't, but I want to learn!" OR "Yes, I can swim, but not very well."

STEP 3: YOU DO (Student Alone)

Write 5 sentences about what you CAN and CAN'T do. Use this format:

"I can [ability]. I can [ability]. I can't [inability]."

  1. Ask: "Can you help me with...?" (something you need)

  3. Make a REQUEST: "Can I...?" (asking permission for something)

Examples: "Can you help me with my homework?" / "Can you cook?" / "Can I use your phone?"
