The CALM Framework

When something goes wrong, stay professional. Use this 4-step framework:

C — Clarify the problem. What exactly is wrong?

A — Apologise briefly. Show you understand.

L — Look for a solution. What CAN you do?

M — Move forward. End on a positive note.

Say these 4 steps from memory. Close your eyes first!

Useful Phrases — Step 1: CLARIFY

❓
"I understand there's a problem. Can you tell me what happened?"
Open, professional — shows you're listening
🔍
"I'm sorry, there seems to be a misunderstanding. Let me check that for you."
Acknowledges the issue and shows you'll investigate
💬
"I understand the problem. What I heard is... Is that correct?"
Confirms you understand — repeats back what they said

Useful Phrases — Step 2: APOLOGISE

🤝
"I apologise for the confusion."
Professional, brief — doesn't make excuses
😟
"I'm sorry this happened. It's not the standard we aim for."
Takes responsibility without over-apologising
✋
"You're right to be concerned. I understand why you're frustrated."
Validates their feelings — very powerful for calming people

Useful Phrases — Steps 3 & 4: LOOK + MOVE

🔧
"What I can do is..."
Focus on solutions, not problems
⏱
"I'll sort this out for you by Friday."
Give a specific deadline — shows you're taking action
✅
"I'll email you the corrected information. Is that OK?"
Proposes action and confirms it works for them
🙏
"Thank you for bringing this to our attention. We really value your business."
Ends on respect and gratitude — moves forward

Bad Response vs Professional Response

Look at these pairs. One is unprofessional. One is calm and professional. Which would you rather receive as a customer?

Situation 1: Wrong Price Information

The problem: A visitor was told the price was €50. The real price is €85.

❌ Not Professional

"That's not our problem. The price is €85. My colleague made a mistake, not me."

Why it fails: Blames someone, rejects responsibility, sounds dismissive.

✅ CALM & Professional

"I apologise for the confusion. Let me check our system... Yes, the correct price is €85. What I can do is give you our volume discount — so it comes to €75. Does that work for you?"

Why it works: Clarifies, apologises, offers a solution, moves forward positively.

Which response would make you trust the company more? Why?

Situation 2: Sample Not Ready

The problem: A visitor wants to see a product sample. You promised it, but it's not ready.

❌ Not Professional

"Sorry, it's not here. The warehouse is slow. You'll have to wait."

Why it fails: Makes an excuse, gives no timeline, no solution.

✅ CALM & Professional

"I understand you wanted to see the sample today. I apologise — the warehouse is still processing it. What I can do is send it to you by tomorrow morning, or I can show you detailed photos on my tablet right now. Which would you prefer?"

Why it works: Acknowledges the issue, apologises, offers two solutions.

Tell your teacher: what makes the second response better? What solutions did they offer?

Situation 3: Delivery Delay

The problem: A customer ordered a product for next week. Now you must tell them it's delayed by 2 weeks.

❌ Not Professional

"The delivery is late. It'll arrive on the 15th instead of the 8th. Sorry."

Why it fails: Vague, apologetic but no action plan.

✅ CALM & Professional

"I need to let you know about a delay. Your order will now arrive on 15th March instead of 8th March. I apologise — this isn't what we promised. What I can do is give you a 10% discount on this order, and I'll track the shipment and email you updates. Does that help?"

Why it works: Clear dates, apology, compensation, and ongoing support.

Compare the two responses. What does the professional response do that the weak one doesn't?

Dear Maria,

I need to let you know about a delay with your order. The package should have arrived yesterday, but it's still in transit. I apologise — this is not the service we aim for.

What I can do is track the shipment today and send you updates. You should receive it by Friday. In addition, I'm crediting 10% back to your account for the inconvenience.

Thank you for your patience, and please contact me if you have any questions.

Best regards,
Thomas

Notice: This email has all 4 CALM steps: Clarify (the delay and status), Apologise (briefly), Look (track & credit), Move (thank you + contact).
