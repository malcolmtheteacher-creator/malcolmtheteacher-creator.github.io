💎 Let's Learn: MY, YOUR, HIS, HER

To talk about your family, you need to show who belongs to whom. We use: my, your, his, her

Pattern: My/Your/His/Her + Family Member

Structure: [My/Your/His/Her] + [Family word]

Examples:

  • My father - the father belonging to me
  • Your mother - the mother belonging to you
  • His brother - the brother belonging to him
  • Her sister - the sister belonging to her

🗣️ How to Say It

Mother = MUTH-ur (not "mah-ther")

Father = FAH-thur (stress first syllable)

Brother = BRUTH-ur (not "bro-ther")

Sister = SIS-tur (stress first syllable, short I sound)

Children = CHIL-drun (not "chil-dren"—second syllable is "drun")

My, Your, His, Her Chart

Person Word to Use Example
I My My family is big.
You Your Your brother is kind.
He His His mother lives here.
She Her Her father is a doctor.
⚠️ Common Mistake: "His" and "Her" look the same BUT: his = he/his, her = she/her. Don't confuse them!
