👔 Formal vs. Casual

How you describe experiences changes based on your audience:

💬 With Friends 👔 With Teacher/Boss
So I went to Paris last month and it was awesome! I recently had the opportunity to visit Paris, and it was quite impressive.
While I was walking around, I saw this crazy thing happen. While I was exploring the city, I witnessed something quite remarkable.
I've never experienced anything like it before! I had not previously encountered such an experience.
Then stuff got really interesting... Subsequently, the situation became increasingly fascinating.

Remember: The tenses (past, past continuous, etc.) stay the same, but the vocabulary and expressions change. Casual = simpler words, contractions. Formal = complex words, full forms.

🎯 The Challenge

Native speakers switch between tenses CONSTANTLY when telling stories. A1 students often get stuck on one tense. Here's what good storytellers do:

  • Past: For the main events ("I went to Paris...")
  • Past Continuous: For the background ("While I was walking...")
  • Present Perfect: For recent experiences ("I've never seen anything like it.")
  • Present: For general truths ("People always say...")

The result? Stories that are alive, detailed, and interesting. Not boring single-tense narratives.

Tab 2: Tense Review — All 4 Together

⏰ PAST SIMPLE (Main events)

Form: I went | He walked | They ate

Use: Finished actions in the past

"I went to a concert last week. It started at 7pm. I bought a ticket and found my seat."

🔄 PAST CONTINUOUS (Background/context)

Form: Was/were + -ing (I was walking | They were sitting)

Use: Actions happening when something else occurred

"While I was waiting for the concert to start, the lights went down. People were cheering loudly."

✨ PRESENT PERFECT (Recent experiences)

Form: Have/has + past participle (I've been | She's seen)

Use: Recent or life experiences that connect to now

"I've never heard them live before. My friend has told me they're amazing, and now I understand why!"

📖 PRESENT SIMPLE (General truths in stories)

Form: I go | They like | He plays

Use: Habits, facts, even in past stories for effect

"The venue holds 10,000 people. Concerts there are always packed. People say it's the best acoustics in the city."

Tab 3: Storytelling Expressions

Speakers use these 5 expressions to make stories flow and feel natural:

1️⃣ First/To begin with

Use: Start the story

"First, I arrived at the station. / To begin with, I was nervous."

Try it:

2️⃣ Then / After that / Next

Use: Connect events in sequence

"Then the train arrived. / After that, we boarded. / Next, I realized I'd forgotten my ticket."

Try it:

3️⃣ While / During / Meanwhile

Use: Show things happening at the same time

"While I was looking for my seat, my friend was buying snacks. / During the show, people were singing along."

Try it:

4️⃣ Suddenly / All of a sudden

Use: Dramatic moment (surprise twist!)

"Suddenly, the power went out. / All of a sudden, I heard someone shout."

Try it:

5️⃣ In the end / Finally

Use: Finish the story

"In the end, it was one of the best concerts ever. / Finally, we went home exhausted but happy."

Tab 4: Model Story Analysis

A Travel Story (Full Example)

First, I decided to visit my cousin in Barcelona. I booked a flight (PAST SIMPLE) and paid €50 (PAST SIMPLE) for the ticket.

The day I arrived, while I was walking from the airport (PAST CONTINUOUS), I saw the beach for the first time (PAST SIMPLE). My cousin was waiting for me (PAST CONTINUOUS) at the station.

I've never been to Spain before (PRESENT PERFECT), and it was incredible (PAST SIMPLE). Barcelona is known for its architecture (PRESENT SIMPLE), and I could finally understand why.

Then, we've spent the week exploring (PRESENT PERFECT) the city. We walked down Las Ramblas (PAST SIMPLE) and ate fresh seafood (PAST SIMPLE).

Suddenly, it started raining (PAST SIMPLE), but we were having so much fun (PAST CONTINUOUS) that we didn't care. Good memories are made in any weather (PRESENT SIMPLE).

In the end, I have had (PRESENT PERFECT) the most amazing trip. I'll never forget it.

COLOUR KEY:

  • Yellow = PAST SIMPLE
  • Green = PAST CONTINUOUS
  • Orange = PRESENT PERFECT
  • Blue = PRESENT SIMPLE

Tab 5: Tell Your Own Story

Choose one experience and tell it using all 4 tenses:

📝 Story Checklist

  • Start with "First" or "To begin with" — set the scene (Past)
  • Use "Then" or "While" — describe what happened (mix tenses)
  • Use "Suddenly" — add a moment of interest or surprise
  • Include "I've..." — reference a life experience (Present Perfect)
  • Use "In the end" — finish the story
  • Minimum 100 words

Self-check:

  • Did I use at least 3 different tenses? ✓
  • Did I use connecting words (First, Then, While, Suddenly)? ✓
  • Did I tell a real experience? ✓

Tab 6: Advanced Technique — Describing Feelings & Reactions

The best stories include HOW you felt, not just WHAT happened:

Describing Feelings (Past)

"I was nervous." (Past Continuous)
"I felt anxious." (Past)
"I was excited!" (Past Continuous)

Describing Reactions (with "I've never...")

"I've never felt so happy." (Present Perfect)
"I'd never seen anything so beautiful." (Past Perfect)
"I've never been so embarrassed." (Present Perfect)
