A2

Trade Fair Survival

The essentials — introduce yourself, describe your product, make connections, handle the unexpected

1First Contact & Introductions

Greet visitors, introduce yourself and your company, ask about them.
GREET → COMPANY → PRODUCT → CONNECT

2Describing What You Offer

Explain your product clearly so visitors understand and ask the right questions.
W-W-W: WHAT → WHY → WHO

3Exchanging Contacts

End conversations well, swap details, and set up follow-ups.
EXIT: End → eXchange → Invite → Thank

4Small Talk & Socialising

Make comfortable conversation at dinners, breaks, and networking events.
SAFE: Surroundings · Activities · Food · Events

5Handling Problems

Deal with misunderstandings and complaints calmly and professionally.
CALM: Clarify → Apologise → Look → Move

6Full Fair Simulation

All 5 frameworks together in realistic trade fair simulations.

B1/B2

Confident Communicator

Pitch, persuade, negotiate, network, handle objections, and follow up like a pro

1The Elevator Pitch

Craft a memorable 30-second pitch that makes the right people remember you.
HOOK → SOLVE → PROVE

2Features vs Benefits

Turn product specifications into compelling reasons to buy.
FAB: Feature → Advantage → Benefit

3Negotiating at the Fair

Protect your margins while keeping the relationship strong.
PREP: Position → Reason → Evidence → Proposal

4Strategic Networking

Build genuine business relationships — not just a pile of business cards.
GIVE: Genuine · Identify · Value · Exit

5Handling Objections

Turn tough questions into your competitive advantage.
FEEL → FELT → FOUND

6Follow-Up Communication

Turn fair conversations into contracts with effective follow-up.
STAR: Specific → Thank → Action → Remind
C1

Strategic Leader

Persuade, adapt, negotiate, present, manage crises, and turn fairs into long-term results

1Strategic Pitching & Persuasion

Make your message land with different stakeholders every time.
AIDA: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action

2Cross-Cultural Communication

Adapt your style for an international audience — differences become advantages.
BRIDGE: Be aware · Read · Interpret · Discuss · Go · Expect

3Leading Negotiations

Move promising conversations to signed agreements.
BATNA · ZOPA · Strategic Concessions

4Presentations at Trade Events

Deliver compelling presentations that make your expertise your brand.
SPARK: Story → Problem → Approach → Results → Kickstart

5Crisis Management

Handle high-stakes conversations with composure and leadership.
LEAD: Listen → Empathise → Assess → Decide

6Post-Fair Strategy

Turn trade fair conversations into long-term business results.
IMPACT: Immediate · Measure · Prioritise · Analyse · Create · Track
