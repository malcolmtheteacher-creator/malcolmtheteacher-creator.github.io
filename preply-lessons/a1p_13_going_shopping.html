üõçÔ∏è Why Shopping Language Matters

You use these phrases every time you buy something:

Scenario 1: You walk into a coffee shop.

You: "Can I have a coffee, please?"

Shopkeeper: "Sure! That's 3 pounds."

You: "Here you are. Do you have any cake?"

Shopkeeper: "Yes, we have chocolate cake and apple cake."

You: "How much is the chocolate cake?"

Shopkeeper: "2 pounds and 50 pence."

Scenario 2: You're at a market with a friend.

You: "How much are these bananas?"

Seller: "5 pounds a kilogram."

You: "Can I have one kilo, please?"

Seller: "That's 5 pounds."

You: "Here's a 10-pound note. Do you have change?"

Seller: "Yes, here's 5 pounds."

Notice the phrases? "Can I have...", "How much is/are...", "Do you have...", "Here you are" ‚Äî these are the KEY SHOPPING PHRASES.

üí¨ The Main Shopping Phrases

Learn these 5 essential expressions:

Can I have...?
What it means: Asking to buy something
Example: "Can I have a coffee, please?" / "Can I have 2 bottles of water?"
How much is/are...?
What it means: Asking about price
Example: "How much is the coffee?" / "How much are the apples?"
Do you have...?
What it means: Asking if the shop has something
Example: "Do you have milk?" / "Do you have any bread?"
Here you are.
What it means: Giving money to the shopkeeper
Example: (You give money) "Here you are." / "Here's 10 pounds."
Do you have change?
What it means: Asking if the shopkeeper can give you smaller money back
Example: "Here's 20 pounds. Do you have change?" (You expect money back)

These 5 expressions cover 90% of real shopping situations!

üí∞ Prices & Money Phrases

You also need to understand numbers and money!

How to say prices:

  • ¬£3 = "Three pounds"
  • ¬£3.50 = "Three pounds fifty" OR "Three fifty"
  • ¬£0.99 = "Ninety-nine pence"
  • ¬£10.20 = "Ten pounds twenty"

Money conversations:

  1. "That's 5 pounds." (The price)
  2. "Here's 10 pounds." (You give money)
  3. "Your change is 5 pounds." (Money back to you)
  4. "Do you have change for 20?" (Asking for small bills)
