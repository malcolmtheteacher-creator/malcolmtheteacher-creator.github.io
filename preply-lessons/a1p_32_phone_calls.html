üìû The Phone Call Framework (A.M.I.)

Three Steps to Phone Confidence

A = ANSWER & IDENTIFY - Who am I? Who are you? Why are you calling?
M = MESSAGE OR REASON - What's this call about? What do you need?
I = IDENTIFY NEXT STEPS - What happens now? Should I call back? Leave a message?

Why A.M.I. works: Without seeing faces, you need to be CLEAR about who you are and what you want. This framework keeps you organized.

üìû Step 1: ANSWER & IDENTIFY

üü¢ When YOU answer the phone:

"Hello?" / "Hi, this is [Your Name] speaking" / "Yes, hello?"

üü¢ When YOU call someone:

"Hi, is this [Person's Name]?" / "Hello, could I speak to [Name]?"

üü¢ If the person is not there:

"Could you tell them I called?" / "When will they be back?" / "May I leave a message?"

üí¨ Step 2 & 3: MESSAGE & NEXT STEPS

üü¢ Leaving a voicemail message:

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name]. I'm calling about [reason]. Could you call me back at [number]? Thanks!"

üü¢ Taking a message for someone else:

"I'm sorry, she's not here right now. Can I take a message?" / "May I ask who's calling?"
Voicemail Pattern:
  1. Say your name clearly
  2. Say why you're calling
  3. Say your phone number SLOWLY
  4. Say "please call me back" or "I'll try again later"

Answer: "Must" (very important) and "should" (advice). Example: "You should call back tomorrow."
