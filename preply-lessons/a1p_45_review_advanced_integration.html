👔 Formal vs. Casual — Comprehensive Review

By now, you understand that English changes based on context. Here's how your A1 skills transform across registers:

Skill 💬 Casual (Friends) 👔 Formal (Teacher/Boss)
Starting conversations Hey, what's up? Tell me more! Hello. I'd like to hear more about that.
Telling stories So like, I went there and crazy stuff happened! I had the experience of visiting, and several remarkable events occurred.
Past experiences I've never done that before, bro! I have not had that experience previously.
Making invitations Wanna come to my party? Would you be interested in attending my gathering?
Declining invitations Nah, can't make it. Sorry! I regret that I am unable to attend.
Asking for clarification Huh? Say that again? Could you please clarify that for me?

Key insight: The GRAMMAR (tenses, structures) stays exactly the same. What changes is:

  • Vocabulary: "cool" vs. "pleasant" | "stuff" vs. "situation"
  • Contractions: "I've" (casual) vs. "I have" (formal)
  • Sentence length: Short & direct (casual) vs. longer & complex (formal)
  • Politeness markers: "please," "would you," "I appreciate"
  • Tone: Relaxed & friendly vs. respectful & professional

📚 Present Perfect Deep Dive [+3 days - Lesson 43]

Rule: Use when asking "Have you ever...?" or "I've never..."

Form: Have/Has + past participle

Complete with Present Perfect:

1. I (see) this movie three times.

2. She (live) in Paris since 2015.

Check answers →
✓ Answers:
  1. I have seen this movie three times.
  2. She has lived in Paris since 2015.

Why? Present Perfect connects a past action to the present moment. Both indicate experiences or states that continue from past to now.
