🗺️ Let's Learn: Countries & Locations

When people ask "Where are you from?" they're asking about your country or city. Here are some countries with the pattern for talking about them.

Pattern: "I'm from [country]"

Structure: I + am (I'm) + from + country name

Examples:

  • "I'm from Brazil." 🇧🇷
  • "I'm from Japan." 🇯🇵
  • "I'm from Canada." 🇨🇦
  • "I'm from Spain." 🇪🇸
  • "I'm from South Korea." 🇰🇷

🗣️ How to Say It

From = FRUM (not "frome"—silent E at end)

Brazil = bruh-ZIL (stress on second syllable)

Japan = juh-PAN (stress on second syllable)

Canada = KAN-uh-duh (three syllables, stress first one)

Thailand = TY-land (stress on first syllable, one word)

🎌 Let's Learn: Nationalities

When you say "I'm from [country]", people might ask "What nationality are you?" Here's the pattern:

Pattern: "I'm [nationality]."

Structure: I + am (I'm) + nationality adjective

Examples:

  • "I'm Brazilian." (from Brazil) 🇧🇷
  • "I'm Japanese." (from Japan) 🇯🇵
  • "I'm Canadian." (from Canada) 🇨🇦
  • "I'm Spanish." (from Spain) 🇪🇸
  • "I'm French." (from France) 🇫🇷
  • "I'm German." (from Germany) 🇩🇪
  • "I'm Thai." (from Thailand) 🇹🇭
  • "I'm Turkish." (from Turkey) 🇹🇷

⚠️ Important: Country ≠ Nationality

Country (the place): "France" → Nationality (describing people): "French"

Country (the place): "Germany" → Nationality (describing people): "German"

Country (the place): "Brazil" → Nationality (describing people): "Brazilian"

💡 Tip: You can say BOTH: "I'm from Brazil" AND "I'm Brazilian" — they mean the same thing and both are correct!
