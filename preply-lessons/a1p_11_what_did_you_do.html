üìö How to Make Regular Past Verbs

Rule: Base verb + -ED = Past tense

The Verbs We'll Learn Today:

WORK
‚Üí WORKED
I worked yesterday. / She worked hard.
WALK
‚Üí WALKED
I walked to school. / He walked home.
WATCH
‚Üí WATCHED
I watched TV. / We watched a movie.
COOK
‚Üí COOKED
I cooked breakfast. / She cooked dinner.
LISTEN
‚Üí LISTENED
I listened to music. / They listened carefully.

Why -ED?

In English, most regular verbs show the past by adding -ED. It's the most common way to talk about something that already happened. Only a few "irregular" verbs (like GO ‚Üí WENT, EAT ‚Üí ATE) don't follow this rule ‚Äî we'll learn those later.

ü§ù Guided Practice: I Do ‚Üí We Do ‚Üí You Do

üéØ Interleaved Practice

Mix it up! Below are 8 sentences. Some are present, some are past. You complete them with the correct form.

1. I _____ (walk) to school every day.

Present or Past?

WALK (present) ‚Äî "every day" = now

2. Yesterday, I _____ (cook) dinner.

COOKED (past) ‚Äî "Yesterday" = already happened

3. She _____ (listen) to music right now.

LISTENS (present) ‚Äî "right now" = this moment

4. Last week, he _____ (work) every day.

WORKED (past) ‚Äî "Last week" = finished

Last week, he worked every day.

5. They _____ (watch) TV together every evening.

WATCH (present) ‚Äî "every evening" = regular habit

They watch TV together every evening.

6. I _____ (walk) home yesterday.

WALKED (past) ‚Äî "yesterday" = already happened

I walked home yesterday.

7. We _____ (listen) to the teacher every day.

LISTEN (present) ‚Äî "every day" = regular

We listen to the teacher every day.

8. You _____ (cook) breakfast this morning.

COOKED (past) ‚Äî "this morning" = already finished

You cooked breakfast this morning.
