üìÖ Why Future Language Matters

Look at the TIMELINE:

PAST
I worked
NOW
I work
FUTURE
I am going to work

Examples of future plans:

  • "I'm going to visit my family next week."
  • "We're going to have lunch tomorrow."
  • "She's going to start a new job next month."
  • "They're going to move to a new house soon."

Notice: These are PLANS, DECISIONS, or THINGS YOU'VE ALREADY DECIDED TO DO.

In this lesson, you'll learn to make sentences about the future using "going to" ‚Äî the most natural way to talk about plans!

üìö The "Going To" Formula

The structure is simple:

Subject + BE (am/is/are) + GOING TO + Base Verb
I am going to work. / She is going to visit. / They are going to eat.

Let's break it down with pronouns:

I am going to work
You / We / They are going to work
He / She / It is going to work

Examples with different verbs:

  • I am going to walk to the park
  • She is going to eat dinner at 7
  • They are going to watch a movie
  • We are going to visit London

IMPORTANT: After "going to", you ALWAYS use the BASE FORM of the verb (work, eat, watch) ‚Äî NOT the past form (worked, ate, watched).

‚è∞ Time Words for the Future

Use these time words to show WHEN you're going to do something:

Tomorrow

Next day

"I'm going to walk tomorrow."

Next week / month / year

Future time period

"She's going to visit next week."

Soon

Not specific, but near future

"I'm going to eat soon."

In 5 minutes / 2 hours / 3 days

Specific time distance

"We're going to leave in 5 minutes."

This weekend

The upcoming weekend

"I'm going to rest this weekend."

Later

Vague future

"I'm going to call you later."

PATTERN: "I'm going to [VERB] [TIME]" OR "[TIME], I'm going to [VERB]"

Both are correct! "Tomorrow I'm going to sleep late" or "I'm going to sleep late tomorrow"
