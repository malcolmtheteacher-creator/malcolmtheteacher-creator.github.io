The First Conditional: If + Present, Will + Future

Learning Intention: Today we're learning the first conditional (If + present, will + future) so you can talk about real possibilities and natural consequences.
üß† What do you already know?

Try writing 2-3 sentences about what will happen if something occurs. For example: "If I study hard, ..."

üìñ Why This Matters

The first conditional describes real possibilities ‚Äî things that could happen. In English, we use this structure every day:

  • "If I wake up early, I'll exercise."
  • "If it rains tomorrow, we'll stay inside."
  • "If you don't finish homework, your teacher will be unhappy."

It's not fantasy ‚Äî it's talking about likely outcomes in your daily life.

üîß The Form: If + Present, Will + Verb

Structure:

If clause (present simple) Main clause (will + verb)
If I wake up early I will exercise
If it rains we will stay home
If you don't study you won't pass
Key Points:
  • IF clause uses present simple (not future): "If I go" NOT "If I will go"
  • Main clause uses will/won't: "I will do" or "I won't do"
  • You can start with either: "If it rains, I'll stay home" OR "I'll stay home if it rains"

üéØ Interleaved Practice: Mix old + new

These exercises mix the first conditional with past tense and present simple. YOU choose which structure:

1. If you _____________ (arrive) late, your boss _____________ (be) angry.

Answer: "If you arrive late, your boss will be angry."
First conditional: present + will

2. They _____________ (go) to the beach yesterday because it _____________ (be) sunny.

Answer: "They went to the beach yesterday because it was sunny."
Past simple (not first conditional).

3. If the weather _____________ (not improve), the match _____________ (be cancelled).

Answer: "If the weather doesn't improve, the match will be cancelled."
First conditional with negation.

Question: What's the difference between "I'm going to..." and "I will..."?

Answer: "Going to" = plans already made. "Will" = decisions in the moment or predictions.

From Lesson 28: Have You Ever...?

Question: What tense is "Have you ever...?" and what does it mean?

Answer: Present perfect. It asks about life experiences from the past to now.
