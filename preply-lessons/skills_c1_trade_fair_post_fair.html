The IMPACT Framework: Post-Fair Strategy

The fair ends. You're exhausted. You have business cards, notes, promises, and a list of follow-ups. Now what?

IMPACT is your framework for turning fair activity into real business.

Immediate Follow-Up
Measure Results
Prioritise Leads
Analyse Performance
Create Action Plan
Track Progress

1. IMMEDIATE FOLLOW-UP (Within 48 hours)

Goal: Strike while memory is warm.

Contacts cool fast. A follow-up email two weeks later is forgotten. Two days later is remembered.

  • "Within 48 hours..." — is your internal rule
  • "I promised to send you..." — fulfil every small promise immediately
  • "Our conversation at the fair got me thinking..." — personal reference, not template
  • One personal email beats ten generic ones

2. MEASURE RESULTS (Week one)

Goal: Know what actually happened.

  • Leads generated: How many genuine prospects vs. business cards for business cards' sake?
  • Conversations: Deep dives vs. surface chats?
  • Partnership opportunities: Any serious collaborations?
  • Brand visibility: Did you solve a problem in someone's mind? (Even if no immediate sale.)

3. PRIORITISE LEADS (Week one)

Goal: Not all leads are equal. Sort ruthlessly.

  • Tier 1: Ready to move forward — Active interest, timeline clear, budget implied, decision-maker met
  • Tier 2: Curious, not ready — Interested but no immediate timeline, still exploring
  • Tier 3: Long-term pipeline — Good fit but problem not urgent, check in every 3 months
  • Tier 4: Archive — Lovely people, wrong fit, no follow-up needed

4. ANALYSE PERFORMANCE (Week two)

Goal: What worked? What didn't?

Questions to ask yourself:

  • Which conversations went deepest? Why?
  • What opening line actually worked to stop people?
  • When did people say "no thanks" — and why?
  • Which framework did you use most? (AIDA, BRIDGE, BATNA, SPARK, LEAD?)
  • What surprised you? What would you do differently next time?

5. CREATE ACTION PLAN (Week two)

Goal: Turn insight into action.

  • "Within one week, I will contact all Tier 1 leads and propose a next meeting."
  • "By the end of the month, I will have closed at least one small deal."
  • "Every Tier 2 lead gets a check-in email once a month."
  • Assign responsibility. Set deadlines. Make it real.

6. TRACK PROGRESS (Ongoing)

Goal: Make the ROI of the fair visible.

  • Revenue closed from fair leads: How much?
  • Pipeline created: How many qualified prospects for next quarter?
  • Brand: How many people now know about you who didn't before?
  • Relationships: Did you deepen any existing partnerships?

The Complete 10-Minute Fair Simulation

Now you're going to run through a complete trade fair day in compressed form. This draws on everything: AIDA, BRIDGE, BATNA, SPARK, LEAD.

Scenario: You work for a B2B software company. Your booth is at a tech fair. You'll encounter four situations across 10 minutes. Your job is to navigate all of them.

Step 1: A Visitor Approaches (2 minutes)

The Scene: A person stops at your booth. They look interested but don't know much about you. You have maybe 90 seconds before they decide to move on.

Your Task: Use AIDA to open. Grab Attention (surprising statement or question), show them it's relevant to their Interest, make them want to know more.

Step 2: They Raise an Objection (2 minutes)

The Scene: You've explained what you do. They say: "Your solution looks interesting, but it's expensive compared to the alternative, and I don't know if the ROI justifies it."

Your Task: Use BRIDGE thinking. What's their world? (They're cost-conscious, uncertain about value.) How do you acknowledge that and move them?

How do you use BATNA thinking here? What's their best alternative?

Step 3: They Want a Presentation (2 minutes)

The Scene: They're interested. They ask: "Can you do a quick demo? But I only have 5 minutes."

Your Task: Use SPARK. You have 5 minutes. Story → Problem → Approach → Results → Kickstart. Make it count.

Step 3: 2 minutes (prep for 5-min demo)

Step 4: A Crisis Interrupts (2 minutes)

The Scene: Mid-conversation, someone else arrives. They say: "Can I interrupt? We're a competitor and we're hearing concerns about your product from mutual clients. Your support team hasn't been responsive. What's going on?"

Your Task: Use LEAD. Listen first. Don't defend. Empathise. Handle the pressure while the original prospect watches.

Step 4: 2 minutes

The Real Debrief: Speaking Like a Team

After the fair, your team debriefs. This is spoken, not written. It's a conversation where you extract learning and decide next steps.

Debrief Questions (15-minute conversation):

  1. Overall: "What's one thing that surprised you about the fair?"
  2. Pitches: "Which conversation went deepest? What made that different?"
  3. Objections: "What did people ask that we weren't ready for?"
  4. Frameworks: "Which framework did you actually use? When did it help?"
  5. Crises: "Did anything go wrong? How did we handle it?"
  6. Next steps: "Who are our three most important follow-ups, and why?"

Not all follow-ups are equal. Here's how to sort them:

Active interest + clear timeline. These could close in 30 days.

Tier 2: Within Two Weeks

Interested but no immediate timeline. Monthly touchpoint.

Tier 3: Quarterly Check-In

Good fit, dormant. Plant a seed and wait.

Lovely people, wrong fit. Save energy for hot leads.

Top 10 Phrases from the Entire Course

These phrases have come up across all six lessons. They work because they're authentic, specific, and client-focused. Master these:

1. "What keeps you awake at night?"

Opens with empathy. Moves past surface features to real concerns.

2. "I want to make sure I fully understand your concern..."

Buys time. Shows respect. Prevents defensiveness.

3. "Let me take you back to..."

Opens with story. Immediately creates connection.

4. "The question we should all be asking is..."

Reframes thinking. Makes them curious.

5. "Here's what the data tells us..."

Grounds emotion in evidence. Adds credibility.

6. "I can see this is important to you..."

Acknowledges emotion. Doesn't dismiss it.

7. "If you take away one thing today, it's this..."

Lands your core message. Makes it memorable.

8. "I take full responsibility for..."

Rebuilds trust in crisis. Rare. Powerful.

9. "What would it take for you to move forward?"

Unlocks their criteria. Moves toward closure.

10. "Within 48 hours, I'll send you..."

Creates momentum. Shows you respect their time.

Why These Work

They all do the same thing: they focus on the OTHER person. They don't sell. They listen, understand, and help them see what's possible. Use them naturally, not as scripts.

Full Course Reflection: How Far You've Come

You can now walk into any trade fair with confidence. You know how to pitch persuasively, bridge difference, negotiate closing positions, present with impact, manage crises, and turn conversations into real business. Most importantly, you know that expertise is your brand.

All Six Frameworks at a Glance:

AIDA (Lesson 1 - Strategic Pitching): Attention → Interest → Desire → Action. The four stages of persuasion. Use this when you have 2-3 minutes and a clear ask.

BRIDGE (Lesson 2 - Cross-Cultural): Background → Respect → Identity → Goals → Environment. How to connect across difference. Use this when you're meeting someone from a different culture or world.

BATNA/ZOPA (Lesson 3 - Negotiation): Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement + Zone of Possible Agreement. Understand what they can walk away to, and where you both win. Use this in real negotiations where money or terms are on the table.

SPARK (Lesson 4 - Presentations): Story → Problem → Approach → Results → Kickstart. For 10-minute stage presentations. Use this when you're speaking to a group on behalf of your expertise.

LEAD (Lesson 5 - Crisis): Listen → Empathise → Assess → Decide. For high-stakes, emotionally charged conversations. Use this when trust is broken or conflict is rising.

IMPACT (Lesson 6 - Post-Fair): Immediate Follow-Up → Measure → Prioritise → Analyse → Create Plan → Track. Your 6-week roadmap after the fair. Use this to turn fair activity into real revenue.
