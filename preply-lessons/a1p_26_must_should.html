Today we're learning: Modal verbs MUST (strong obligation) and SHOULD (advice/recommendation) to express what is necessary and what is a good idea.

üéØ When Do We Use These?

MUST: Something is necessary or required

  • "You must go to school." (It's a rule/law)
  • "You must see this film!" (Strong recommendation)

SHOULD: Something is a good idea or advice

  • "You should drink more water." (Advice)
  • "You should exercise." (Recommendation)

By the end, you'll express obligations and give advice confidently.

How to Use MUST & SHOULD

Pattern: Modal + Base Verb (No -S, No -ED)

Modal Meaning Strength Examples
MUST Necessary/Required Very Strong ‚ö°‚ö°‚ö° I must go. They must study.
SHOULD Good idea/Advice Moderate ‚ö°‚ö° You should rest. She should call.

Negative Forms

Positive Negative Example
I must go I must not go (mustn't) You mustn't be late.
I should rest I should not (shouldn't) You shouldn't work too hard.
‚úì Important Rule:

Modal + INFINITIVE (base verb, no -S)

‚úì Correct: "You must go" NOT "You must goes"

‚úì Correct: "I should study" NOT "I should studies"
