Master the art of asking questions

What = WHAT (one syllable, short A)

When = WEN (one syllable, short E)

📖 Why Questions Matter

Questions are how you CONNECT with people. Whether you're making small talk, solving a problem, or having a real conversation, you need to ask questions naturally. This lesson brings together everything you've learned about forming questions across different tenses and structures.

🎯 The Four Main Question Types

1️⃣ YES/NO QUESTIONS (Do...? Is...? Are...?)

"Do you like coffee?" / "Is it raining?" / "Will you come tomorrow?"
Pattern: [Auxiliary] + [Subject] + [Verb] + [Rest]?

2️⃣ WH-QUESTIONS (What, Where, When, Why, Who, How)

"What time is it?" / "Where do you live?" / "Why did she leave?"
Pattern: [WH-word] + [Auxiliary] + [Subject] + [Verb] + [Rest]?

3️⃣ MODAL QUESTIONS (Can, Could, Would, Should)

"Can you help?" / "Would you like coffee?" / "Could you wait?"
Pattern: [Modal] + [Subject] + [Verb] + [Rest]?

4️⃣ QUESTIONS WITH DIFFERENT TENSES

Present simple: "Do you work here?"
Past simple: "Did you go yesterday?"
Present continuous: "Are you working now?"
Present perfect: "Have you finished?"
🔧 Word Order: The Golden Rule
For YES/NO questions:
✓ "Do you like it?" (Auxiliary BEFORE subject)
✗ "You do like it?" (Wrong order)

For WH-questions:
✓ "What do you do?" (WH + Auxiliary + Subject)
✗ "What you do?" (Missing auxiliary)

For MODALS:
✓ "Can you help?" (Modal BEFORE subject)
✗ "You can help?" (Wrong — sounds like a statement)
👨‍🏫 I DO (Model)
I'm forming a question about what you did yesterday:
"What did you do yesterday?"

Breaking it down: [WH-word: What] + [Auxiliary: did] + [Subject: you] + [Verb: do]
Why: Past simple needs "did", so "did" comes BEFORE the subject.
👥 WE DO (Together)

Form a question: Present continuous — "Where are you going?"

Breakdown: [WH: Where] + [Auxiliary: are] + [Subject: you] + [Verb: going]
Present continuous uses "are", which goes BEFORE the subject in questions.

🎯 Interleaved Practice

Each question requires you to CHOOSE the right form. Mix of question types and tenses:

1. Form a question to ask if someone is coming tomorrow (future with will):

" you tomorrow?"

Answer: "Will you come tomorrow?"

2. Form a yes/no question in present:

" they live in London?"

Answer: "Do they live in London?"

3. Form a modal question (Can/Could):

" you help me?"

Answer: "Can you help me?" or "Could you help me?"

4. Form a WH-question about when (past):

"When you home?"

Answer: "When did you come home?"
💬 Questions in Real Conversations

Questions don't exist in isolation — they flow in actual conversations. See how to use questions naturally:

Example 1: Meeting someone new

Person A: "Hi, I'm Alex. Do you live here?"
Person B: "Yes, for 5 years. Where are you from?"
Person A: "I'm from Spain. Have you ever been there?"
Person B: "No, but I'd love to! What's it like?"

Notice: Yes/no question → WH-question → Present perfect → WH-question. Natural flow!
Example 2: Asking about plans

Person A: "Are you coming to the party?"
Person B: "When is it?"
Person A: "Saturday at 8. Can you make it?"
Person B: "I'm not sure. What time should I arrive if I do?"

Notice: Yes/no → WH- → Yes/no → WH-. Each answer leads to the next question.
