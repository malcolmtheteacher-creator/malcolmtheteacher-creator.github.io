IELTS Writing Task 2

Opinion Essays (Agree / Disagree)  ·  B2–C1

What is an Opinion / Agree-Disagree essay?

The prompt asks for your view on a statement or claim. You must decide whether you agree, disagree, or partly agree — and then develop a clear, supported argument. This is not a discussion of "both sides" — you take a position and defend it.

Recognising the question type

Typical prompt patterns:

▸ "To what extent do you agree or disagree with this statement?"

▸ "Do you agree or disagree? Give your opinion and include relevant examples."

▸ "Some people believe… Others argue… What is your view?"

Structure: Two common approaches

A
Full agreement / disagreement (cleaner, safer)
Intro (state position clearly) → Body 1 (reason 1 + support) → Body 2 (reason 2 + support) → Conclusion (restate position). Both body paragraphs support the same side.
B
Partial agreement (more nuanced, higher risk)
Intro (state partial position) → Body 1 (concede opposing point) → Body 2 (your main argument — stronger point) → Conclusion (reaffirm your position). Your opinion must dominate, not balance.

Which approach should you choose?

If you are confident in your ideas and language, approach B can score higher for sophistication. However, many students lose marks by appearing to give a balanced view rather than a personal position — this fails the task achievement criterion. If in doubt, commit to one side fully (approach A) and develop your arguments deeply. A well-developed full agreement essay scores higher than a half-hearted partial one.

Think about this:

Some students try to avoid giving a strong opinion in an opinion essay. Why might they do this? What risk does it create in IELTS?

Is it possible to strongly disagree with a statement even if you personally agree with it in real life? Should you?

Writing with a neutral academic tone

Opinion essays ask for your view — but that doesn't mean informal language. IELTS rewards formal, measured writing. Compare these examples.

✗ Too casual / weak

"I really think that community work is a great thing and everyone should do it."

✓ Formal and measured

"I strongly believe that community service plays a vital role in building social cohesion and should be encouraged more widely."

✗ Too absolute

"Advertising to children is totally wrong and should be completely banned immediately."

✓ Measured and academic

"There is a compelling argument that advertising targeting children raises serious ethical concerns and warrants stricter regulation."

Useful phrases for stating your position

I firmly believe that Strong, clear position.
I firmly believe that all young people should be encouraged to volunteer in their local communities.
In my view, / In my opinion, Standard, neutral opinion marker. Use once — don't repeat throughout the essay.
In my view, the benefits of this policy far outweigh the drawbacks.
It is my contention that Formal, academic. Good for the introduction or conclusion.
It is my contention that governments bear primary responsibility for environmental protection.
While there are merits to both positions, I would argue that Acknowledges complexity before committing to a position.
While there are merits to both positions, I would argue that individual responsibility plays a greater role.
You want to say: "I think volunteering is important."
→ Rewrite using a more formal, developed phrase.
I strongly believe that volunteering is an invaluable practice that benefits not only the individual but also the wider community.
You want to say: "Advertising to kids is bad."
→ Rewrite in a neutral, academic register.
It is my contention that commercial advertising directed at young children is ethically problematic and should be subject to greater regulatory oversight.

Discuss:

Can you explain the difference between I think and it is my contention that in terms of register? When would each be appropriate?

Building a strong argument paragraph

Each body paragraph in an opinion essay should follow a clear pattern: state your point → explain why → give support.

1
Topic sentence
State your main point for this paragraph clearly.
2
Explanation
Explain why this point is true or significant. Don't just restate it.
3
Example or evidence
Support with a specific example, statistic, or real-world reference. This shows depth.
4
Link back to position
End with a sentence that connects this point back to your overall argument.

Model argument paragraph

Read this paragraph and identify each of the four steps above.

[A] One significant advantage of requiring young people to participate in voluntary community work is the development of key social and professional skills. [B] When individuals work alongside people from different backgrounds towards a shared goal, they naturally develop communication, teamwork, and problem-solving abilities that are difficult to teach in a classroom setting. [C] In countries such as South Korea and Germany, where some form of national service exists, surveys have shown that participants report stronger civic engagement and professional confidence later in life. [D] This suggests that exposure to voluntary work at a young age has benefits that extend well beyond the immediate task itself, supporting the case for its wider adoption.

[A] Topic sentence → [B] Explanation → [C] Example / evidence → [D] Link back to position

Vocabulary for building arguments

Furthermore / Moreover Add another supporting point.
Furthermore, research indicates that voluntary work improves mental wellbeing.
This suggests that Draw a conclusion from evidence.
This suggests that early exposure to volunteering has lasting benefits.
For instance / For example Introduce a supporting example.
For instance, many community health programmes rely entirely on volunteer networks.
It is widely acknowledged that Present a view as broadly held — useful for adding weight to a claim.
It is widely acknowledged that volunteering fosters empathy and social responsibility.

Discuss:

Think about a topic you feel strongly about — education, technology, work-life balance. Can you build a two-sentence argument using the topic sentence + explanation pattern?

Plan your essay

📝 The prompt:

Some people believe that young people should be required to do voluntary community work. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this view?

Step 1 — Choose your position:

▸ Fully agree — compulsory volunteering benefits everyone

▸ Fully disagree — compulsion undermines the spirit of volunteering

▸ Partially agree — voluntary encouragement is better than compulsion, but the principle is sound

Step 2 — Brainstorm your two main reasons:

Reason 1: Develops social/professional skills — teamwork, communication, resilience through real-world experience.

Reason 2: Strengthens community bonds — young people gain perspective on social needs; helps reduce divisions and build civic identity.

Step 3 — Supporting examples for each reason?

Reason 1 support: Countries with national service programmes show higher rates of civic participation and employment readiness among young adults.

Reason 2 support: Community projects — food banks, environmental clean-ups, elderly care — depend on volunteer networks to function. Young volunteers directly meet real social needs.

Discuss:

Does your country have any form of compulsory national or community service? What is your personal opinion on the idea?
