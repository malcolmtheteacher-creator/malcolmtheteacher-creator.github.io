üéôÔ∏è Shadowing ‚Äî the most powerful pronunciation tool

Shadowing means speaking along with a recording in real time, matching the speaker as closely as possible ‚Äî rhythm, sounds, pitch, everything. It's the fastest way to build natural pronunciation habits.

1

Listen first (no speaking)

Play a short clip (30‚Äì60 seconds). Just listen. Notice the rhythm, the pitch patterns, where the stress falls, and how words connect. Don't try to understand every word ‚Äî feel the shape of the speech.

2

Mumble along

Play it again. This time, mumble along ‚Äî you don't need to say real words. Just match the rhythm, the ups and downs of pitch, the speed. Think of it as humming the melody of the speech.

3

Shadow with the recording

Now speak along with the recording in real time. Don't pause it. Stay as close to the speaker as possible ‚Äî about half a second behind. Match their rhythm, their connected speech, their intonation. It's okay to stumble.

4

Shadow without the recording

Try to reproduce the same passage from memory ‚Äî or from the transcript. Keep the same rhythm, connected speech, and intonation. This is where the real learning happens.

5

Record and compare

Record yourself. Play it back. Compare with the original. What's close? What's different? Focus on one specific feature to improve, then try again.

üéØ The golden rule of shadowing: Don't try to fix everything at once. Each time you shadow, pick ONE feature to focus on ‚Äî rhythm, vowels, consonants, connected speech, or intonation. Do five rounds, each with a different focus.

üß† Lesson 1 ‚Äî The Rhythm Engine

1. English is stress-timed. What does this mean in practice ‚Äî and how does it affect how fast function words are said?

Stress-timed means stressed syllables occur at roughly equal intervals, regardless of how many unstressed syllables sit between them. Function words (the, to, have, was, of) get compressed and reduced to fit between beats ‚Äî that's why they sound like /√∞…ô/, /t…ô/, /…ôv/, /w…ôz/ in natural speech. The rhythm drives the weak forms.
