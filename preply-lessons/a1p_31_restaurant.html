üéØ The Restaurant Framework (R.A.B.)

Three Steps to Restaurant Success

R = REQUEST - What do you want? How do you ask politely?
A = ADDRESS PROBLEMS - If something's wrong, how do you complain without being rude?
B = BILL & GOODBYE - How do you ask for the bill and thank them?

Why R.A.B. works: It gives you three clear moments in a restaurant conversation. You know WHAT to do and WHEN to do it.

üìã Step 1: REQUEST (Ordering Politely)

There are THREE ways to ask for food. Each one is polite, but they're used at different times:

üü¢ For menu items:

"Can I have the pasta, please?" / "I'll have the grilled chicken, please."

üü¢ For drinks:

"Could I get a coffee, please?" / "May I have a glass of water?"

üü¢ For questions about food:

"Does this dish have nuts?" / "Is the fish fresh today?" / "What comes with the salad?"

üòü Step 2: ADDRESS PROBLEMS (Complaining Politely)

Sometimes food isn't perfect. Here's how to complain WITHOUT being rude:

üî¥ POOR way (too direct):

This soup is cold and tastes bad.

üü¢ GOOD way (polite):

I'm sorry, but this soup seems to be cold. Could you possibly bring me a hot one instead?
The Pattern:
  1. "I'm sorry, but..." (shows politeness)
  2. "This [problem] seems to be [what's wrong]" (explain, don't attack)
  3. "Could you possibly...?" (polite request for help)
More examples:
‚Ä¢ "I'm sorry, but I think there's a mistake. I ordered fish, not chicken."
‚Ä¢ "Excuse me, the coffee seems to be cold. Could I get a fresh cup?"
‚Ä¢ "I'm sorry, but this isn't what I ordered. Could we sort this out?"
