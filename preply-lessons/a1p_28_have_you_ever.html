Today we're learning: How to ask and talk about life experiences using "Have you ever...?" and Present Perfect (have/has + past participle).

üéØ Why Present Perfect?

Present Perfect: Something happened in the past, but it connects to NOW

  • "Have you ever eaten sushi?" (Anytime in your life ‚Üí relevant now)
  • "I have traveled to five countries." (Past experiences ‚Üí part of who I am)
  • "She has never tried snow." (Life experience she hasn't had)

By the end, you'll ask about and share life experiences naturally.

How to Form Present Perfect

Pattern: HAVE/HAS + PAST PARTICIPLE

Subject Auxiliary Past Participle Example
I / You / We / They have eaten I have eaten sushi.
He / She / It has eaten She has eaten sushi.

Past Participles - Regular & Irregular

Regular: Base + -ED

  • play ‚Üí played | work ‚Üí worked | study ‚Üí studied

Irregular: Must memorize

  • eat ‚Üí eaten | go ‚Üí gone | see ‚Üí seen | do ‚Üí done
‚úì Correct Pattern:

Subject + have/has + past participle

"I have been to Paris." "She has never tried ice cream."
