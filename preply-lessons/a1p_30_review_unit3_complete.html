💭 L21 - Right Now! (Present Continuous) [+3 days]

Task: Complete this sentence: She (read) the book right now.

Hint: Think "be + verb-ing"

✓ Correct Form: She is reading the book right now.

Why? Present Continuous = am/is/are + -ing. Use for actions happening RIGHT NOW.

💭 L22 - Bigger & Better (Comparatives) [+3 days]

Task: Complete: A dog is (intelligent) than a cat.

Hint: Think "more/less + adjective"

✓ Correct Form: A dog is more intelligent than a cat.

Why? Comparative = more/less + long adjective (OR short adjective + -er). Use to compare TWO things.

💭 L23 - Giving Opinions [+7 days]

Task: Speak about 3 expressions for introducing an opinion:

✓ Examples: "I think..." / "I believe..." / "In my opinion..." / "If you ask me..."

Why? These are signal phrases that show you're sharing an opinion, not a fact. Essential for expressing yourself!

💭 L24 - The Best! (Superlatives) [+7 days]

Task: Complete: Everest is (high) mountain in the world.

Hint: Think "the + [most/least] + adjective" or "the + adjective + -est"

✓ Correct Form: Everest is the highest mountain in the world.

Why? Superlative = the + most/least + adjective (OR the + adjective + -est). Use when comparing 3+ things OR saying something is #1.

💭 L26 - Must & Should (Modal Verbs) [+14 days]

Task: What's the difference between MUST and SHOULD? Speak about 1 sentence for each.

✓ Key Difference:
  • MUST = Strong obligation (you HAVE to do it). Example: "You must wear a seatbelt."
  • SHOULD = Advice/recommendation (it's a good idea). Example: "You should study harder."

💭 L27 - Telling Stories with Sequencing [+14 days]

Task: List 3 sequencing words for stories (in order):

✓ Sequencing Words (Timeline Order):
  1. First (at the beginning)
  2. Then / Next (what happens after)
  3. Suddenly (unexpected twist)
  4. Finally / In the end (conclusion)

💭 L28 - Have You Ever? (Present Perfect) [+21 days]

Task: Complete: I (never/try) sushi.

Hint: Think "have/has + past participle"

✓ Correct Form: I have never tried sushi.

Why? Present Perfect = have/has + past participle. Use for life experiences OR to connect the past to NOW. "Have you ever...?" is a classic question form.

💭 L29 - Making Suggestions [+21 days]

Task: Speak about 3 ways to make suggestions. Label each as casual, polite, or formal:

✓ Three Levels of Formality:
  • Casual: "Let's go!" / "Wanna come?"
  • Polite: "Why don't we...?" / "How about we...?"
  • Formal: "Shall we...?" / "I would suggest..."

Key skill: Matching your suggestion to the situation!

Integrated Skills Challenge (Interleaved Practice)

Real-world scenarios combining MULTIPLE structures (not just one topic). This mirrors real communication!

Why Interleaving Works: In real life, you don't use ONE grammar rule at a time. You mix them. This practice develops flexible thinking.

Scenario 1: Comparing & Expressing Opinions [Interleaving L22 + L23]

Your friend says: "I like summer more than winter."

Your task: Disagree politely. Your response MUST include:

  • ✓ A comparative (using bigger/better/more/less/happier)
  • ✓ An opinion phrase ("I think...", "In my opinion...")
  • ✓ A reason with "because"

Model Answer: "I see what you mean, but I think winter is more beautiful because it's quieter and the snow is amazing."

Scenario 2: Storytelling with Sequencing [Interleaving L27 + L28]

Tell a short story about something that happened to you.

Your story MUST include:

  • ✓ Time marker to set the scene (Yesterday, Last week, When I was 10...)
  • ✓ 3 events using sequencing words (First... Then... Suddenly... Finally)
  • ✓ At least ONE "I have never..." or "Have you ever?" reference

Model Answer: "Last week, I had a strange day. First, I missed my bus. Then, I walked to work and met an old friend. Suddenly, it started raining! I have never had such a crazy morning!"

Scenario 3: Advanced Interleaving [L21 + L22 + L26 + L29]

You want to suggest a group activity to your friends.

Your suggestion MUST include:

  • ✓ Describe what's happening right now (Present Continuous: "We're standing here...")
  • ✓ Make a suggestion (Let's/Why don't we/Shall we)
  • ✓ Compare it to another activity (Comparative: "...is more fun than...")
  • ✓ Give a reason using must/should (Modal verb)

Model Answer: "Right now we're just standing around doing nothing. Why don't we go to the park? It's more interesting than sitting here, and we should get some fresh air. Come on!"
