üó∫Ô∏è Real Direction Conversations

You're in a new city. You're lost. You need to ask a local person for help:

Real situation:

YOU: "Excuse me! Can you help me? Where is the train station?"

LOCAL: "Yes! Go straight. Turn left at the traffic light. It's on your right."

YOU: "Thank you! Is it far?"

LOCAL: "No, it's 5 minutes from here."

In this conversation, you use:

  • A question: "Where is...?"
  • Imperatives: "Go straight", "Turn left" (commands)
  • Prepositions: "on your right", "on the left", "at the corner"

This is 100% practical English ‚Äî you'll use it when traveling!

‚ùì How to ASK for Directions

Learn these 5 ways to ask:

Where is the [place]?
What it means: Asking the location of something
Example: "Where is the station?" / "Where is the restaurant?"
How do I get to [place]?
What it means: Asking for the route/directions
Example: "How do I get to the hotel?" / "How do I get to the hospital?"
Is it far from here?
What it means: Asking about distance
Example: "Is the supermarket far?" (usually answered with "5 minutes away")
Can you help me? I'm lost.
What it means: A polite way to start asking for help
Example: "Can you help me? I'm looking for the train station."
Do you know where [place] is?
What it means: Polite way to check if they know
Example: "Do you know where the library is?"

‚û°Ô∏è How to GIVE Directions (Imperatives)

When giving directions, use IMPERATIVE verbs (commands):

Go straight.
Continue in the same direction
"Go straight for 2 minutes."
Turn left / Turn right.
Change direction (90 degrees)
"Turn left at the corner." / "Turn right at the traffic light."
It's on your left / right.
The place is beside you
"The caf√© is on your left." / "The bank is on your right."
Cross the street.
Go to the other side of the road
"Cross the street. The museum is there."
It's next to / opposite [place].
Location descriptions
"The pharmacy is next to the supermarket." / "It's opposite the park."

Complete example:

"Go straight for 5 minutes. Turn left at the traffic light. Go straight again. The station is on your right, next to the caf√©."
