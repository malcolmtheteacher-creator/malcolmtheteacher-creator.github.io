👶 Lesson 46: Used To

A1 Pathway Transition Unit (A1→A2) • Knowledge Building

🗣️ How to Say It

Used to = YOOST tuh (two words, stress first one, "used" sounds like "yoost")

Habit = HAB-it (two syllables, stress first one)

Past routine = PAST roo-TEEN (stress both words)

No longer = noh LONG-ur (stress second word)

Ago = uh-GOH (two syllables, stress second one)

Today we're working on: The "used to" structure for past habits and routines that changed — so you can talk about how you've changed over time.

👔 Formal vs. Casual

How you talk about past habits depends on your audience:

💬 With Friends 👔 With Teacher/Boss
I used to love playing football every day. I previously had a regular practice of playing football.
I didn't use to like coffee, but now I do! My preferences regarding coffee have shifted over time.
Did you use to go to school nearby? Did your educational institution happen to be in the vicinity?
We used to hang out a lot back then. We spent considerable time together during that period.

Remember: The grammar (used to structure) is the same for both formal and casual. The difference is in how much detail you give and how simple or complex your vocabulary is.

🔑 The Core Idea

Used to + verb: Something you did regularly in the past but don't do anymore.

"I used to play tennis every week." (past habit, not true now)
"We used to live in London." (past reality, changed)
"She didn't use to like coffee." (past, but now she does)

Tab 2: Form Focus

AFFIRMATIVE NEGATIVE QUESTION
I used to play football I didn't use to play football Did you use to play football?
She used to work here She didn't use to work here Did she use to work here?

⚠️ IMPORTANT RULE: Used to has NO -d in negative/questions!

✗ "I didn't used to..." (WRONG)
✓ "I didn't use to..." (CORRECT)
✗ "Did you used to...?" (WRONG)
✓ "Did you use to...?" (CORRECT)

Practice: Speak about the negative forms:

1. I used to smoke → I smoke.

2. We used to visit them → We visit them.
