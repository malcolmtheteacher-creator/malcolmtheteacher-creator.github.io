The moment to say goodbye well

At trade fairs, conversations end quickly. A visitor looks at their watch. Another person wants to talk to you. It happens. The question is: how do you end the conversation so they WANT to hear from you again?

Have you ever had a conversation end and thought "I wish I could contact them again, but I don't have their details"? What happens?

The EXIT Framework: End the conversation well

EXIT stands for four simple steps:

E
END — The conversation is finishing. Signal this politely.
X
eXchange — Ask for (and give) contact details.
I
Invite — Say you'll send something or stay in touch.
T
Thank — Thank them for their time.

Useful Phrases — E: END the conversation

⏰
"I don't want to keep you any longer."
Politely signals the conversation is ending — shows you respect their time
👋
"It's been great talking to you."
Warm, positive ending — makes them feel the conversation mattered
🎯
"I think we could work well together."
Suggests future business — natural reason to exchange contacts

Useful Phrases — X: eXchange contact details

💼
"Let me give you my business card."
Direct, simple — standard at trade fairs
📧
"Can I have your email address? I'd like to stay in touch."
Personal and professional — if you don't have cards, ask for email
📱
"What's the best way to contact you?"
Gives them control — shows you respect their preference
📇
"Can I have your card?"
Short and natural — the standard request at trade fairs

Useful Phrases — I: Invite them to follow up

📮
"I'll send you our catalogue after the fair."
A concrete next step — they know what to expect
💬
"Let's keep in touch. I'll email you next week."
Shows you'll take action — puts the responsibility on you, not them
🤝
"I'll send you some more information about our products."
Specific promise — they remember what you'll send
📞
"Maybe we can have a call next month to discuss this further?"
Suggests a future meeting — turns the fair into the start of a conversation

Useful Phrases — T: THANK them

🙏
"Thank you so much for your time."
Classic and warm — ends the conversation on a positive note
😊
"Thank you for your interest in our company."
Formal but genuine — acknowledges their engagement
⭐
"It was great meeting you!"
Personal and friendly — leaves them with a smile

🔍 I DO — A model goodbye

Listen to how Marco ends a conversation at a trade fair:

"Thank you so much for your time today. I think we could work well together. Let me give you my business card — and can I have yours? I'll send you our full product catalogue by email tomorrow, and maybe we can have a call next week to discuss this further?"

Notice: Marco ended politely, exchanged cards, promised follow-up, and thanked the visitor. The conversation didn't really end — it moved to the next step.

👆 Click to find all 4 steps of EXIT in Marco's goodbye

E — END: "Thank you so much for your time today."

X — eXchange: "Let me give you my business card — and can I have yours?"

I — Invite: "I'll send you our full product catalogue by email tomorrow, and maybe we can have a call next week."

T — THANK: Already done at the start, which is natural in English.

✍ Write a Follow-Up Email

You have the visitor's email. Now write a short follow-up email — the kind you'll send within 24 hours after the fair. Maximum 4 sentences. Keep it friendly and professional.

3:00

Subject: Following up from the Trade Fair

"Dear Sophia,

It was great to meet you at the fair yesterday. I'm sending you our full product catalogue as promised — I think the PremiumPack will be perfect for your business.

Let's have a call next week to discuss how we can work together. I'll email you some time options.

Looking forward to our conversation!
Best regards,
James"

Notice: Personal (uses name, mentions conversation), honest (promises real next step), professional (clear sign-off). This keeps the connection alive.
