☀️ Lesson 36: Talking About Weather

Master weather vocabulary and small talk

Learning Intention: Today we're learning weather vocabulary and expressions so you can talk about the weather and start simple small talk conversations.

🗣️ How to Say It

Weather = WETH-ur (two syllables, stress first one)

Raining = RAY-ning (two syllables, stress first one)

Snowing = SNOH-wing (two syllables, stress first one)

Sunny = SUN-ee (two syllables, stress first one)

Cloudy = KLOWD-ee (two syllables, stress first one)

🧠 What do you already know?

Say out loud 5 weather words you already know in English (rain, sun, snow, etc.)

☀️ Why Weather Matters

"Isn't it hot today?" / "Terrible weather we're having!" / "Beautiful day, isn't it?"

Weather is THE universal small talk. On buses, waiting rooms, coffee shops, elevators — strangers bond over weather. It's the safest topic that makes you sound natural and engaged.

🌧️ Weather Types & Expressions
☀️ Sunny / Clear: "It's sunny today" / "Clear skies" / "Bright sunshine"
🌧️ Rainy / Wet: "It's raining" / "It's pouring" / "Rainy weather" / "Wet"
❄️ Cold / Snow: "It's freezing" / "It's cold" / "Snowing" / "Icy roads"
💨 Windy: "It's windy" / "Breezy" / "Strong winds"
☁️ Cloudy: "Overcast" / "Cloudy" / "Grey skies"

💬 Weather + Opinions (Describing How Weather Feels)
Positive: "Beautiful day!" / "Perfect weather" / "Lovely sunshine" / "Gorgeous"
Negative: "Terrible weather" / "Awful rain" / "Miserable cold" / "Horrible wind"
Impact on you: "It's so hot I can't work" / "The cold is unbearable" / "Perfect for a walk"
Small talk formulas:

"Isn't it [adjective + weather]?"
"What [terrible/beautiful] weather!"
"I [love/hate] this [weather type]"

💬 Guided Dialogue Practice: How Small Talk Flows

See how weather conversations actually work — they're short exchanges, not long monologues.

Example 1: At a bus stop (sunny day)

Person A: "Isn't it hot today?"
Person B: "Yes! Perfect for the beach."
Person A: "Exactly. I love this weather!"

Notice: Short turns, agreement, natural flow.
Example 2: In a shop (rainy day)

Person A: "Terrible weather, isn't it?"
Person B: "I know. I got soaked coming here."
Person A: "Me too. At least it will cool things down."

Notice: Sympathy, common experience, quick resolution.
Example 3: With a friend (cold day)

Person A: "Brr, it's freezing!"
Person B: "I know. Did you see the snow?"
Person A: "Yes, schools might close tomorrow."

Notice: Exclamation, question, prediction (natural flow).
