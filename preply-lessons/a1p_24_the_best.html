Today we're learning: How to express superlatives - the BEST, the BIGGEST, the MOST interesting - when comparing three or more things.

üéØ Why Superlatives Matter

Superlative = ONE thing in a group

  • Comparative: "A lion is stronger than a cat" (comparing 2 things)
  • Superlative: "A lion is the strongest animal" (in a group of animals)

By the end, you'll identify and use superlatives correctly.

Two Patterns for Superlatives

Pattern 1: Short Adjectives + -EST

Adjective Comparative Superlative Example
big bigger the biggest The elephant is the biggest animal.
fast faster the fastest The cheetah is the fastest animal.
tall taller the tallest The giraffe is the tallest animal.

Pattern 2: Long Adjectives + MOST

Adjective Comparative Superlative Example
interesting more interesting the most interesting That's the most interesting film.
expensive more expensive the most expensive Gold is the most expensive metal.
beautiful more beautiful the most beautiful This is the most beautiful place.
‚úì PATTERN:

THE + Superlative + Noun

The tiger is the fastest cat in the world.
