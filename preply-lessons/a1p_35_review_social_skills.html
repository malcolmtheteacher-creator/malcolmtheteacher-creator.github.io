From Lesson 29: Making Suggestions

Question: What are 3 ways to make a suggestion using "Let's", "Why don't", "Shall"?

Answer: "Let's go", "Why don't we try...", "Shall we...?"

From Lesson 30: Unit 3 Review (Describing & Comparing)

Question: How do you form a comparative? Example: "This is ___ (big) than that."

Answer: Add "-er" for short adjectives (bigger), or "more" for longer ones (more interesting)

From Lesson 31: At the Restaurant

Question: What's the pattern for a polite complaint? Start with "I'm sorry, but..."

Answer: "I'm sorry, but [problem]. Could you possibly [solution]?" ‚Äî Shows politeness without attacking.

From Lesson 32: Making Phone Calls

Question: What are the key parts of a good voicemail message?

Answer: Name, reason for calling, phone number (slowly), what they should do next

From Lesson 33: First Conditional

Question: Write one first conditional sentence from memory:

Answer: Any correct sentence following: If + present simple, will + verb (Example: "If I study, I will pass")

From Lesson 34: Questions

Question: What's wrong with this question? "Where you are going?" ‚Äî Fix it.

Answer: Should be "Where are you going?" ‚Äî The auxiliary "are" must come BEFORE the subject.
