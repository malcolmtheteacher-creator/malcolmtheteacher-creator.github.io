Comparative Adjectives

Comparing Things

What Do You Know?

Today we're learning: How to compare two things using comparative adjectives (bigger, faster, more interesting).

üéØ Why Comparatives Matter

Comparative: Compares TWO things

  • "A cat is smaller than a dog." (comparing two animals)
  • "English is easier than Chinese." (comparing two languages)
  • "This phone is more expensive than that one." (comparing two phones)

By the end of this lesson, you'll compare things confidently using both -ER and MORE patterns.

Two Patterns for Comparatives

Pattern 1: Short Adjectives + -ER

Adjective Comparative Example
big bigger A house is bigger than a car.
fast faster A plane is faster than a car.
tall taller John is taller than Mary.
old older My father is older than my mother.

Pattern 2: Long Adjectives + MORE

Adjective Comparative Example
interesting more interesting Films are more interesting than books.
expensive more expensive Gold is more expensive than silver.
beautiful more beautiful Roses are more beautiful than daisies.
important more important Health is more important than money.
‚úì Correct Pattern:

Subject + BE + Comparative + THAN + Object

A phone is smaller than a computer.
