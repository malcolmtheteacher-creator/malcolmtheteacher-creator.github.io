Form: I think + CLAUSE

üëÇ Pronunciation: "I THINK" (stress on THINK)

2Ô∏è‚É£ "I believe..." (Stronger opinion)

Form: I believe + CLAUSE

Examples:

  • I believe technology is important.
  • I believe exercise is healthy.

Note: More formal than "I think"

  • In my opinion, travel is valuable for education.

Reacting to Opinions

4Ô∏è‚É£ "I agree." (Positive response)

Usage: Someone says opinion ‚Üí You say "I agree"

Examples:

  • Person A: "Coffee is delicious."
  • Person B: "I agree!"

You can also say: "That's right!" or "You're right!"

5Ô∏è‚É£ "I disagree." / "I don't agree." (Polite disagreement)

Usage: Someone says opinion ‚Üí You disagree politely

Examples:

  • Person A: "Winter is the best season."
  • Person B: "I disagree. I prefer summer."

Important: Add your opinion after! Don't just say "no".

6Ô∏è‚É£ "I see what you mean, but..." (Partial disagreement)

Usage: You understand BUT have a different view

Examples:

  • Person A: "Smartphones are bad for children."
  • Person B: "I see what you mean, but they're also educational tools."

This shows respect for their opinion while offering another view.
