This is the single biggest consonant difference between British and American English. In RP, you only pronounce /r/ before a vowel sound. If the R comes before a consonant or at the end of a word, it disappears.

1. Explain the non-rhoticity rule. When DO you pronounce R in British English?

Only pronounce R before a vowel sound. Before a consonant or at the end of a word, R is silent. "Red" (R before vowel = say it), "car" (R at end = drop it), "car engine" (R links to the next vowel = say it).
