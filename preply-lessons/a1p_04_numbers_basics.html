üî¢ Let's Learn: Tens & Beyond (20-100)

After 20, numbers follow a clear pattern. Once you know this pattern, you can count to 100:

Pattern: TENS (20, 30, 40...)

  • 20 = twenty
  • 30 = thirty
  • 40 = forty
  • 50 = fifty
  • 60 = sixty
  • 70 = seventy
  • 80 = eighty
  • 90 = ninety
  • 100 = one hundred

Pattern: Combining Tens + Ones

Rule: Tens + ones = combine them!

  • 21 = twenty-one
  • 35 = thirty-five
  • 48 = forty-eight
  • 67 = sixty-seven
  • 92 = ninety-two
‚ö†Ô∏è Careful: "Forty" is 40, not "fourty" ‚Äî Common spelling mistake!

üì± Let's Learn: Phone Numbers & "I'm [age]"

Two practical uses of numbers: sharing a phone number and saying your age:

Saying Your Phone Number üì±

How to do it: Say each digit individually, slowly.

Example phone: 555-1234

How to say it: "Five-five-five, one-two-three-four"

Sentence: "My phone number is five-five-five, one-two-three-four."

Note: Say 0 as "zero" or "oh"

Another example: 202-555-0192

"Two-zero-two, five-five-five, zero-one-nine-two"

Saying Your Age üéÇ

Pattern: "I'm [number] years old" or simply "I'm [number]"

Examples:

  • "I'm twenty-three years old."
  • "I'm thirty-seven."
  • "I'm forty." (simple and natural)
üí° Tip: People usually DON'T say the full "years old" part ‚Äî just say "I'm thirty" and people understand it's your age!
