📊 The Big Picture: Three Tenses

You now know three ways to talk about time:

TENSE FORM WHEN TO USE EXAMPLE
Present Base verb (I work) Every day, habits, now "I work every day."
Past Verb + -ED or irregular (worked, went) Already finished, yesterday "I worked yesterday." / "I went home."
Going To (Future) Be + going to + verb (am/is/are going to work) Plans, decisions, tomorrow "I'm going to work tomorrow."

How to CHOOSE the right tense:

  • Is it happening NOW or EVERY DAY? → Use PRESENT
  • Did it ALREADY HAPPEN? → Use PAST (regular -ED or irregular)
  • Is it a FUTURE PLAN? → Use "GOING TO"

PAST IRREGULAR VERBS you learned:

GO→went | EAT→ate | SEE→saw | COME→came | GET→got | SLEEP→slept | TAKE→took | MAKE→made | DRINK→drank | LEAVE→left

These don't follow the -ED rule — you must memorize them!

🎯 Interleaved Practice: MIX ALL THREE TENSES

Below are 10 spoken responses. Complete them — you decide WHICH TENSE to use!

1. Every day, I (eat) breakfast.

eat — Present (every day)

2. Yesterday, I (eat) pizza.

ate — Past irregular (yesterday)

3. Tomorrow, I (go) to the park.

am going to go — Future (tomorrow)

4. Last week, she (work) hard.

worked — Past regular (last week)

5. She (watch) TV every night.

watches — Present (every night)

6. He (visit) his family next month.

is going to visit — Future (next month)

7. We (sleep) 8 hours last night.

slept — Past irregular (last night)

8. They (make) dinner tomorrow.

are going to make — Future (tomorrow)

9. I (listen) to music every morning.

listen — Present (every morning)

10. They (come) to visit yesterday.

came — Past irregular (yesterday)

Notice: You HAD to CHOOSE which tense was right for each sentence. That's the skill!
