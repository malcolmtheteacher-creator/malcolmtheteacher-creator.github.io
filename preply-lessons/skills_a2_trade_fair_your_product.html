The W-W-W Framework: Describe Any Product or Service

When a visitor asks about what you offer, give them three things:

W
WHAT — What is it? (name + one sentence)
W
WHY — Why is it good? (the benefit — what problem does it solve?)
W
WHO — Who is it for? (your typical client or market)

Simple. Clear. The visitor gets all the key information in 30 seconds.

Useful Phrases — WHAT (What is it?)

📦
"Our main product / service is..."
Simple opening — name the product or service
🔧
"It's a [type] that [what it does]."
Example: "It's a system that tracks deliveries in real time."
📋
"We also offer..."
To mention a second product or service

Useful Phrases — WHY (Why is it good?)

💡
"The main benefit is..."
Example: "The main benefit is that it saves time."
✅
"It helps [people] to [do something]."
Example: "It helps managers to organise their teams."
⭐
"What makes it special is..."
Use for your unique selling point — what competitors don't have

Useful Phrases — WHO (Who is it for?)

🎯
"It's designed for [type of client]."
Example: "It's designed for small and medium businesses."
🌍
"Our clients are mainly [description]."
Example: "Our clients are mainly retailers in Central Europe."
📈
"We work with companies in [sector / region]."
Shows your market and experience
Close your eyes. Can you remember the 3 W's? Say them: the first W is... the second W is... the third W is...

Activate — Handling Questions

The questions visitors always ask

After you describe your product, visitors will ask questions. Here are the 5 most common — and how to answer them at A2 level.

"How much does it cost?"
💰
"The price depends on [quantity / size / package]. I can send you a price list."
"Do you deliver to [country]?"
🚚
"Yes, we deliver to [countries/regions]." / "At the moment, we deliver mainly to..."
"Do you have a catalogue / brochure?"
📄
"Yes, here you are." / "I can email it to you — can I have your card?"
"What's the minimum order?"
📊
"The minimum order is [number/amount]." / "For a first order, we're flexible."
"Can I see a sample?"
🔍
"Of course — here's a sample." / "I can send you a sample after the fair."

🆘 When you don't understand

At a trade fair, people speak fast. Different accents. Technical words. You WILL need these:

Rescue Phrases
🔄
"Sorry, could you say that again?"
🐌
"Could you speak a little slower, please?"
🤷
"What do you mean by [word]?"
✋
"Let me check and get back to you."
When you don't know the answer — honest and professional
Practise the rescue phrases. Say each one aloud — naturally, not reading. Imagine someone just asked you something you didn't understand.

🔀 Mixed Practice — Fix the descriptions

Each description below has a problem. What's wrong? How would you fix it?

Description 1: "Our product is the best. It's amazing. Everyone loves it. You should buy it."

What's the problem? How would you improve it?
👆 Click to check

Problem: No WHAT, no WHY, no WHO. Only opinions ("best", "amazing") — no facts. The visitor learns nothing. Fix: name the product, explain what it does, give one real benefit, say who it's for.

Description 2: "Our VistaBoard is a digital display system with 4K resolution and LED backlighting. It uses the RS-485 protocol and has a 178-degree viewing angle with 5000:1 contrast ratio."

What's the problem? How would you improve it?
👆 Click to check

Problem: Too technical. Good WHAT, but no WHY and no WHO. The visitor doesn't understand the benefit. Fix: keep the name, but replace the tech specs with a benefit: "It's a digital display that's easy to read even in bright sunlight — perfect for shops and restaurants."

Description 3: "We help businesses to save money. Our clients are very happy. We work in many countries."

What's the problem? How would you improve it?
👆 Click to check

Problem: No WHAT. What's the product? What's the service? "Help businesses save money" is too vague — every company says this. Fix: name a specific product or service first, then explain how it saves money.
