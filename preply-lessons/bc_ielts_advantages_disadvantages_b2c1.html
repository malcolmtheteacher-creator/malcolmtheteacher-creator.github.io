IELTS Writing Task 2

Advantages & Disadvantages Essays  ·  B2–C1

What is an Advantages & Disadvantages essay?

You are given a trend, development, or policy and asked to discuss its positive and negative sides. You do not give your personal opinion — you present a balanced analysis.

Recognising the question type

These prompts all call for an A&D essay — even if the exact wording varies:

Typical prompt patterns:

▸ "Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of..."

▸ "What are the benefits and drawbacks of..."

▸ "Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?" (variant — requires a clear position)

The four-paragraph structure

Introduction
Paraphrase the prompt. Briefly state that there are both advantages and disadvantages to discuss.
Do not copy the question word for word — rephrase it.
Paragraph 1 — Advantages
Give two main advantages, each developed with an explanation or example. Use a topic sentence to signal the focus.
Aim for two clearly separate points, not one point restated twice.
Paragraph 2 — Disadvantages
Give two main disadvantages with supporting detail. Use contrast language to show you are moving to a different perspective.
Balance the paragraph lengths roughly equally with the advantages paragraph.
Conclusion
Summarise both sides in one or two sentences. Do not introduce new information or arguments here.
Paraphrase your introduction — do not repeat it word for word.

Think about this:

How is an A&D essay different from an opinion essay? When might the examiner penalise you for sharing your own view in this type of essay?

Why do you think it is important to give two points in each body paragraph, rather than just one?

Analyse a model outline

Look at this IELTS prompt and the student's planning notes. Then answer the questions below.

📝 The prompt:

Nowadays, technology is increasingly used in schools and universities. Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of this development.

A student's planning notes — do these make a strong essay?

Introduction: Paraphrase → technology is now a central part of education at all levels.

Advantage 1: Up-to-date information — technology gives students access to the latest research, news, and learning materials, unlike traditional textbooks which quickly become outdated.

Advantage 2: Access anytime — students can study at their own pace outside school hours, which is particularly useful for revision and independent learning.

Disadvantage 1: Impact on grades — the presence of personal devices in class can be distracting; students may use them for social media rather than learning.

Disadvantage 2: Addiction risk — excessive screen time can create dependency, making it difficult for students to focus without a device.

Conclusion: Both sides summarised — no new information added.

1. Does the outline balance advantages and disadvantages equally?

Yes — the student has given two advantages and two disadvantages. This shows balance and helps achieve a higher score for coherence and cohesion.

2. Are the points specific or vague? Give one example of a well-developed point.

The points are specific. For example, Advantage 1 doesn't just say "better information" — it explains why technology is better (real-time updates) and contrasts it with textbooks going out of date. This development earns marks for task achievement.

3. Does the conclusion add a new idea or summarise? Is that the right approach?

The conclusion summarises — which is correct. Introducing a new argument in the conclusion is a common mistake and can lower your coherence and cohesion score. Always paraphrase and sum up; never add new content in the final paragraph.

Language for contrast and concession

Strong IELTS essays use a variety of linking words to signal contrast. Click each card to see how it works, then practise using it.

Despite Used before a noun or gerund (-ing).
Despite the benefits of technology, many students struggle to self-regulate.
While / Whilst Introduces a contrast mid-sentence.
While technology gives access to information, it can also be highly distracting.
However Connects two separate sentences. Place a comma after it.
Technology offers many benefits. However, these come with significant risks.
On the other hand Signals a shift to the opposite viewpoint. Use at the start of a sentence or paragraph.
On the other hand, excessive screen time may damage concentration skills.
Although / Even though Introduces a concession — acknowledges the opposing point before making yours.
Although there are clear advantages, the disadvantages should not be ignored.
Nevertheless Means "despite what was just said". More formal than however.
Technology improves access to resources. Nevertheless, not all students can afford devices.

Transformation practice

Rewrite each pair of ideas as one sentence using the word in brackets. Check your answer when ready.

Technology improves access to learning materials. / It can be distracting in class.
→ Use: While
While technology improves access to learning materials, it can be distracting in class.
Students benefit from anytime access to content. / There is a risk of device addiction.
→ Use: Despite
Despite the benefit of anytime access to content, there is a risk of students developing device addiction.
Technology keeps textbooks up to date. / Many schools cannot afford the latest devices.
→ Use: Although
Although technology keeps learning materials up to date, many schools cannot afford the latest devices to use it.

Discuss:

Which of these linking words is most formal? Which would you most naturally use in spoken English? Does that surprise you?

The "outweigh" variant

Some prompts ask whether the advantages outweigh the disadvantages. This changes the task — you must present both sides and give a clear personal position.

Key difference from the standard A&D essay:

In a standard A&D essay — you balance both sides neutrally.
In an "outweigh" essay — you still cover both sides, but your conclusion states which side is stronger and why. This is your opinion.

📝 Outweigh prompt:

Governments in many countries now provide free internet access to all citizens. Do the advantages of this policy outweigh the disadvantages?

Brainstorm: What are the advantages of free government internet?

Suggested advantages:
▸ Ease of access — citizens can use government services, healthcare, and education online without financial barriers.
▸ Equality of access — reduces the digital divide between higher and lower income households; gives everyone the same opportunity to learn and participate in the digital economy.

Brainstorm: What are the disadvantages of free government internet?

Suggested disadvantages:
▸ Reliance on the internet — communities may lose the ability to function without connectivity; essential services become vulnerable to outages.
▸ Misinformation — free, unrestricted access increases exposure to false information, propaganda, and online fraud, particularly for less digitally literate citizens.

Planning your position

Use the space below to plan a response to the outweigh prompt. Decide: do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages? Write a one-sentence thesis that you would put at the end of your introduction.

Discuss:

If you were a government minister, would you support free public internet access? Use at least one piece of contrast language in your answer.
