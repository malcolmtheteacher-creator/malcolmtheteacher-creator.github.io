📌 Lesson 18 - Frequency Adverbs (Habits)

Q: I (usually) drink coffee in the morning.

✓ Answer: I usually drink coffee in the morning.

Review: Frequency words go AFTER "be" but BEFORE other verbs.

Order from most to least: always → usually → often → sometimes → rarely → never

📌 Lesson 21 - Present Continuous (What's Happening Now)

Q: Change to present continuous: "I study right now."

✓ Answer: I am studying right now.

Review: Present Continuous = am/is/are + verb-ing

Use for: actions happening NOW, ongoing situations, temporary activities

📌 Lesson 21 - Present Continuous vs Present

Q: Which is correct? "I eat lunch" vs "I am eating lunch" (happening NOW)

✓ Answer: "I am eating lunch" (happening right now)

Review:

Present = habits, facts, routines

Present Continuous = actions happening now, temporary situations

📌 Lesson 22 - Comparatives (Bigger & Better)

Q: Complete: A car is (fast) than a bicycle.

✓ Answer: A car is faster than a bicycle.

Review: Comparative structure = adjective + ER (or MORE + adjective)

Short adj (1-2 syllables): add -ER (fast→faster)

Long adj (3+ syllables): use MORE (important→more important)

📌 Lesson 23 - Giving Opinions

Q: Speak about 4 ways to start an opinion sentence:

✓ Answer Examples:

1. "I think..."
2. "I believe..."
3. "In my opinion..."
4. "I feel that..."

Review: After opinion phrases, you can disagree respectfully:

"I disagree." / "I see your point, but..." / "Actually, I think..."

📌 Lesson 24 - Superlatives (The Best!)

Q: Complete: Mount Everest is (high) mountain in the world.

✓ Answer: Mount Everest is the highest mountain in the world.

Review: Superlative = THE + adjective-EST (or THE MOST + adjective)

Short adj: the + adj-EST (high→the highest)

Long adj: the most + adj (beautiful→the most beautiful)

📚 Lesson 26: Must & Should (Modal Verbs)

New Skill: Express obligation, advice, and recommendations.

  • "You must arrive on time." (obligation - very important)

  • "You shouldn't eat too much sugar." (negative advice)

📚 Lesson 27: Telling Stories (Narrative Tenses)

New Skill: Tell stories about what happened, in order.

  • Review Past for main events: "I went to the beach."
  • Use Past Continuous for background: "It was raining while we played."
  • Use sequencing words: "First... then... after that... finally"

Why this matters: Share memories and experiences naturally

Why this matters: Ask and answer about experiences (important for conversations!)
