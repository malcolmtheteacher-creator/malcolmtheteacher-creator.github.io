👔 Formal vs. Casual — Connected Speech

Fillers and connectors change based on context — but the linking techniques stay the same!

💬 Casual Speech 👔 Formal/Professional
Um, like, I went to the store, you know? Well, I went to the store to purchase some items.
So basically, it was really fun, kind of. In essence, it was quite an enjoyable experience.
Wanna grab coffee? Like, now or later? Would you like to have coffee? Perhaps now or at a later time?
I mean, I didn't wanna go, but, yeah... I must say, I was hesitant about attending, however, I did.

Remember: You can use linking and sound blending in both contexts (gonna, wanna, didja are natural in both). But fillers like "um, like, you know" are casual. In formal settings, use "well, I think, actually, in my opinion."

Key insight: Fluency isn't about perfection. It's about natural rhythm, linking words, and keeping the flow going. Native speakers use CONNECTORS, FILLERS, and LINKING all the time!

Tab 2: Connected Speech Techniques

1️⃣ LINKING WORDS (Connectors)

What it is: Words that join ideas smoothly (so, because, and, but, also, however)

Unnatural: "I went to France. I had fun. I learned French."
Natural: "I went to France and had fun, so I decided to learn French. However, it's harder than I thought."

Linking words to practice: and, but, because, so, also, however, besides, in addition, actually

YOUR TURN: Connect these 2 ideas with a linking word:

"I love coffee. I don't sleep well at night."

2️⃣ FILLERS (Thinking time)

What they do: Buy you time to think without awkward silence

"Um, well, I think that..." / "You know, like, it's really..." / "So, basically, what I mean is..."

Professional fillers: Well, you know, I mean, basically, actually, I think, in my opinion

Casual fillers: Um, like, uh, sort of, kind of, I guess

3️⃣ SOUND LINKING (Pronunciation blending)

What it is: Connecting the sounds of words so they flow

Word by word: "Did you go?" (sounds slow)
Linked: "Didja go?" (sounds natural)

Word by word: "Want to" (sounds careful)
Linked: "Wanna" (sounds natural)

Common linked forms:

  • Did you → Didja
  • Want to → Wanna
  • Going to → Gonna
  • Have to → Hafta
  • Got to → Gotta

Tab 3: Linking in Action

Unnatural vs Natural Speaking

UNNATURAL (word by word):

"I... um... went... to... the... store. And. I. bought. milk. And. I. bought. bread. Because. I. wanted. to. make. dinner."

NATURAL (with linking + fillers):

"Well, you know, I went to the store and got milk and bread 'cause I was gonna make dinner, you know?"

What changed?

  • ✓ Added connectors ("and", "'cause")
  • ✓ Added fillers ("well", "you know")
  • ✓ Linked sounds ("gonna" instead of "going to")
  • ✓ Smoother pace
