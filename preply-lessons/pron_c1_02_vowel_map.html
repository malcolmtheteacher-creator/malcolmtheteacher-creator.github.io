üß† From Lesson 1 ‚Äî The Rhythm Engine (+3 interval)

1. English is described as "stress-timed." What does that actually mean? How is it different from your language?

Stress-timed means the stressed syllables happen at regular intervals (like a beat), and unstressed syllables get compressed between them. Most other languages are syllable-timed ‚Äî every syllable takes the same time.

üß† From Lesson 1 ‚Äî Content vs Function (+3 interval)

2. Name 3 types of content words and 3 types of function words. What happens to function words in natural speech?

Content: nouns, main verbs, adjectives (also adverbs, negatives, question words)

Function: articles, prepositions, auxiliaries (also pronouns, conjunctions)

Function words get reduced ‚Äî quieter, shorter, and their vowels become schwa /…ô/.
