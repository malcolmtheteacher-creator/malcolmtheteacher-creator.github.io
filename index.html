About Me

A philosopher who became a language teacher - or perhaps the reverse

I've spent twenty-five years teaching across four continents, though the geography matters less than the alchemy that happens when clear thinking meets precise language. I've worked in UK grammar schools, Vietnamese universities, Bangkok classrooms, and Brunei training centers - with RMIT, the British Council, CfBT - but what I've really been doing all this time is watching how ideas take shape in words.

Before I focused on English, I taught A-Level Philosophy and Ethics. That background never left me. When I work with students now, yes, we work on grammar, pronunciation, IELTS preparation - the foundations matter. But I'm also helping you think more clearly in a second language, which requires something beyond technical correction. Language is not merely communication; it's cognition externalized.

I also keep what I call a Memory Palace - a vast mental architecture for organizing knowledge through ancient mnemonic techniques. It contains everything from the Dewey Decimal System to Stoic maxims, epistemology to Epicurean wisdom. Some people collect stamps; I collect ways of thinking. I bring these methods into my teaching because memory and meaning are inextricably linked.

I work with people who take their work seriously - whether that's preparing for an exam, improving English for their job, or just wanting to communicate with more clarity and confidence. If you want a teacher who pays attention and takes the work seriously too, let's talk.

IELTS Preparation

You need a score. Let's get it.

Click to view my IELTS strategy guides:

IELTS isn't about English fluency - it's about understanding what examiners want and delivering it under timed conditions. I've prepared hundreds of students for Academic and General Training, and I know where people lose marks: vague thesis statements in Writing Task 2, incomplete descriptions in Task 1, repetitive vocabulary in Speaking, misreading questions in Reading.

We work systematically through each section. Writing gets the most focus because it's where scores typically suffer - I'll teach you essay structures, how to develop arguments with specificity, and what Band 7+ writing actually looks like. Speaking preparation includes practicing the question types, extending answers naturally, and avoiding memorized responses that examiners spot immediately. For Reading and Listening, it's about technique: scanning strategies, prediction skills, and not losing marks to careless errors.
