How This Course Works

Each lesson follows a seven-part structure: Retrieval (what you remember) → Marshall's Story (the history, told through her prose) → The Deeper Truth (what we now know) → Key Dates (encode with your PAO system) → Memory Palace (place images in your room) → Your Turn (reflect and discuss) → Final Retrieval (lock it in).

Content returns at +3, +7, and +14 lesson intervals through spaced repetition in the Retrieval tabs.

About Our Island Story

H.E. Marshall published Our Island Story: A History of England for Boys and Girls in 1905. It tells the story of Britain from Neptune's myth to Queen Victoria in prose so beautiful it reads like a novel. Marshall wrote for children, but her gift was making history unforgettable. We use her words as our narrative voice — and where she was wrong (as all writers of any age must sometimes be), a historian's note gently sets the record straight.
